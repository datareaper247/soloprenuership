## STAGE 1: $0 TO $10K ARR

**Primary constraint**: Validation.

**Kill signals**:
- No paying customer by month 4 → change the hypothesis, not the feature list
- Cold email to demo <0.5% from 100 targeted → message wrong
- Demo to paid <10% → price, objections, or wrong buyer
- D30 retention <40% → product not embedded in workflow
- Month-over-month growth <10% for 2+ months → PMF not found

**The only channel that works**: Direct outreach.
Find 100 people who demonstrably have the problem. Send a 3-line personalized email. Ask for 15 minutes. Sell on the call. CAC: $50-200. This is correct — you are buying signal, not scale.

**What does NOT work and why**:
- SEO: 90-180 day minimum payoff. You'll run out of runway or motivation first.
- Paid ads: No LTV data = irrational bidding. Learn this from customer calls, not ad spend.
- Community soft launch without genuine membership: Flagged as spam unless you're a real community member.
- Cold ProductHunt without audience: 200-500 visitors, 4-10 customers — insufficient.

**Pricing mechanics**:
One payment type only (not both subscription and one-time simultaneously). Decision paralysis reduces conversion.
The Marc Lou anchor: What does your product replace in time or service cost? Show this before the price.
"Replaces 40 hours of setup at $100/hr = $4,000 of value. Your price: $299." ShipFast case.

**Mandatory before proceeding to Stage 2**:
1. Activation milestone identified (specific action correlated with 3-month retention)
2. Time-to-value under 10 minutes for activation milestone
3. D30 retention above 40%

**Weekly rhythm**: 25 hours total. Monday-Tuesday: outreach (2-3 hrs/day). Wednesday-Thursday: building (4-6 hrs/day). Friday: metrics + next week prep. Weekends: off.

## STAGE 2: $10K TO $100K ARR

**Primary constraint**: Repeatability. Can you make acquisition work 10 more times?

**Kill signals**:
- MoM growth <10% for 3 months → channel saturated
- CAC > 1/3 LTV → unit economics broken
- D30 retention <40% → fix retention before acquisition investment
- NPS / "very disappointed" <40% → not at PMF; do not scale
- Support >2 hours/day → self-service investment overdue

**Channel sequencing**:
1. Exhaust primary channel first. Whatever got you to $10K, do more of it.
2. Add bottom-of-funnel SEO at $15-20K MRR — not before.
   Target: "[Product] alternative to [Competitor]", "[Competitor] pricing", "[Problem] software"
   These rank in 3-6 months vs. 12-18 for informational content.
3. Integration marketplace distribution (after primary channel saturation)

**Pricing mechanics**:
Increase price 25-40% once you have 50 paying customers and stable conversion. Kill signal: conversion drops >50% → return to prior price.
Add annual pricing at 20% discount. Target 20-35% of new users choosing annual.
Add 3-tier pricing: Starter / Pro / Business. Price middle tier at what you want most users to pay.

**Retention mechanics to install**:
1. 5-email onboarding sequence over 14 days
2. In-product activation nudge: if no milestone by day 3 → guided walkthrough
3. 7-day inactivity email showing what they missed
4. Power user VIP: top 10% by usage → beta features + direct Slack access

**Weekly rhythm**: 30 hours total. Monday: support triage. Tuesday-Wednesday: core development. Thursday: marketing execution. Friday: metrics + 1 customer call.

## STAGE 3: $100K TO $1M ARR

**Primary constraint**: Systems and channel diversification.

**Documented limit**: Solo operator sustainable ceiling without hiring ≈ $40-60K MRR. Most founders exit or hire before $1M ARR solo.

**Kill signals**:
- MoM growth <8% for 3 months → add new channel
- Rising blended CAC → channel saturation
- D30 retention <50% → product issue
- Support >10% of revenue → systematize or hire
- NRR <90% → churn exceeding expansion

**Channel architecture**:
1. Primary channel: Maintain and optimize
2. Full-funnel SEO: 3-5 pieces/month, 90-180 day lag
3. Affiliate/referral: 20-30% recurring commission, 5-10 active affiliates
4. Integration distribution: integrations with 10K+ users overlapping ICP
5. Owned community: Slack/Discord for power users

**Pricing mechanics**:
Usage-based becomes viable when a clear value metric exists (API calls, documents, words, seats).
ConvertKit: list-size pricing → revenue expanded automatically with customer success.
Aggressively convert monthly to annual. Tactic: announce price increase → offer annual lock-in at current rate.

## COMPETITIVE MOAT ARCHITECTURE

### Moat Type Rankings for Solo Founders

1. **Compliance Moats** — Strongest. 1-3% monthly churn vs. 5-15% for general SaaS.
2. **Workflow Lock-In** — Product becomes non-removable step in daily workflow. Switching requires redesign.
3. **Proprietary Data Network Effects** — Usage improves the product in unreplicable ways.
4. **Integration Network Effects** — Each integration = distribution channel + switching cost.
5. **Community Moats** — Switching means leaving the community, not just the software.

### Compliance Moat (80-90% 6-month retention)

**How to find compliance angles**:
Industries with mandatory recurring documents done in Excel:
- Construction: Prevailing wage certified payroll (Davis-Bacon Act). ~15K qualifying subcontractors filing weekly.
- Healthcare: HIPAA documentation for telehealth encounters
- Financial services: SOC 2 evidence collection for quarterly audit prep
- Government contractors: CMMC compliance. 300,000+ DoD contractor organizations affected

**Build cost**: $0-500 for regulatory lookup APIs. Timeline: 3-6 months.

### Workflow Lock-In

**How to build it**:
Step 1: Map customer's existing 10-step workflow via interviews.
Step 2: Slot your product into steps 3-5 — don't replace all 10 steps.
Step 3: Create product-specific data accumulation (FeedbackPanda teacher templates).
Step 4: Export deterrence without export prevention (data loses structure value outside your system).

**Build cost**: $0 (design decision). Timeline: 6-12 months of data accumulation.

### Proprietary Data Network Effects

**Real small-scale examples**:
- Subscribr (~$30K MRR): YouTube performance benchmarks competitors need 12-18 months to replicate
- FeedbackPanda: Teacher-created templates refined across thousands of uses
- Compliance tools: Regulatory interpretation precedents per jurisdiction

**How to build**:
1. Identify what data customers generate while using your product
2. Aggregate and anonymize into benchmarks or improvements
3. Surface back as insights only users have: "Based on [N] companies using [Product]..."

**Build cost**: $0-$200/month compute. Timeline: 6-18 months.

### Integration Marketplace Distribution

**Top 5 marketplaces by value**:
| Platform | Reach | CAC | Notes |
|---|---|---|---|
| HubSpot App Marketplace | 200K+ users | $50-200 | Newsletter feature on new listings |
| Notion Integrations | 30M+ users | $20-100 | Gallery placement for featured integrations |

## COMPETITOR INTELLIGENCE AUTOMATION

### System 1: Pricing Change Tracking
Tool: Visualping or ChangeTower (free tiers) OR Firecrawl API ($16/mo)
Alert: Any text change on competitor /pricing URL
Response time: <24 hours after detection

Price increase → email best monthly customers to lock in current rate
Price decrease → evaluate positioning, match or articulate differentiation

### System 2: Review Mining for Feature Gaps
Source: G2 + Capterra 1-3 star reviews, top 3 competitors, first 50 reviews each
Tag by: feature complaint / UX pain / support issue / reliability
Frequency trigger: 5+ reviews mentioning same gap → roadmap candidate
Copy mining: Exact customer language on landing pages converts 15-30% better than founder-written copy

### System 3: Displacement Capture Playbook (30-90 day window)

**Warning signals to monitor**:
1. Acquisition announcements (Google Alert: "[competitor] acquired")
2. G2/Capterra review velocity spikes (>20 reviews in a month that averaged 3/month)
3. Reddit migration threads: search "[competitor] alternatives" monthly
4. Competitor changelog gaps: 60+ days without updates = team distracted

**Displacement response (when signal fires)**:
- Day 1: Publish migration guide "[Competitor] alternative: How to migrate in 30 minutes"
- Day 2: Post in relevant communities (genuine, not promotional)
- Days 3-7: Targeted outreach to identifiable competitor customers
- Days 8-30: Paid campaign targeting "[Competitor] alternative" if >200 searches/month

**Real case**: Evernote 2023 price increase + degraded free tier → Notion, Obsidian, Bear all saw measurable traffic from "Evernote alternative" searches. Founders with SEO pages live captured lasting acquisition at $0 CAC.

## PRICING PSYCHOLOGY

### Van Westendorp Price Sensitivity (run before committing to a price point)

4 questions (30+ respondents minimum):
1. "At what price would this be so cheap you'd question its quality?"
2. "At what price is this starting to feel like a good deal?"
3. "At what price is this starting to feel expensive but you'd still consider it?"
4. "At what price would this be too expensive to consider?"

Plot 4 curves. Acceptable range: where "too cheap" crosses "getting expensive" to where "too expensive" crosses "good value."

**Founder insight**: Most founders price in the "too cheap" zone. If first 20 customers paid without negotiating, your price is almost certainly too low.

### Anchoring

Show the cost of the alternative before showing your price.
Format: "Replaces [X] hours at $[rate]/hr = $[total] of value. Your price: $[actual price]."
The ShipFast example: $4,000 anchor → $299 felt cheap. This anchor belongs above the fold.

### Three-Tier Decoy Pricing

- Starter: $29/mo (limited intentionally — makes next tier look compelling)
- Pro: $79/mo (full features — designed as the natural landing point)
- Business: $149/mo (multi-seat — anchors Pro as a deal by comparison)

### Price Increase Playbook (Marc Lou Ladder)

ShipFast actual history: $99 → $149 → $199 → $299
- Announce on Twitter with 7-day warning
- Grandfather all existing customers
- Monitor conversion rate for 2 weeks
- Kill signal: conversion drops >50% from prior level → found the price ceiling

Annual conversion at increase announcement:
Offer existing monthly customers annual lock-in at current rate.
Expect 15-25% to convert. Improves cash flow + reduces future churn simultaneously.

## 10 ASYMMETRIC PRODUCT FEATURES

### Feature 1: Automated Competitor Mention Alerts
Tool: Mention.com + Slack webhook ($0-49/mo)
What: Monitor Reddit, Twitter/X, G2, Capterra for competitor frustrations
Action: Personal founder DM within hours with specific offer
CAC: $0. Window closes in 24-48 hours.

### Feature 2: Value Realization Dashboard (Customer-Facing)
What: Show customers their ROI in their terms. "You generated 47 documents this month, saving ~23 hours."
Impact: Customers who see ROI quantified renew at 2-3x the rate of customers who must calculate it themselves.
1-2% churn reduction = $6-12K preserved ARR at $50K MRR.

### Feature 3: Churn Prediction Scoring
Metric: Track 3 signals per customer — logins/week, core feature uses/week, support tickets
Trigger: Any metric declining 50%+ over 14 days = at-risk alert
Action: Personal founder email converts 20-40% of at-risk customers back
Without signal: you learn about churn at cancellation date. With it: 30-day warning.

### Feature 4: Competitor Migration Wizard
What: One-click import from top 3 competitors (CSV + intelligent field mapping, or direct API)
Build cost: 2-4 weeks engineering per competitor
Impact: Reduces activation barrier from "significant work" to "10 minutes"
Each successful migration = a case study. "Switch from [Competitor] in 10 minutes" = high-converting SEO page.

### Feature 5: Social Proof Generation Engine
Trigger: At the moment a customer achieves a significant outcome, surface "Share this?" with pre-drafted tweet
Include: Customer's specific result in the template
Reference: HeadshotPro users sharing LinkedIn headshots = organic word-of-mouth → $300K MRR peak
Mechanism: Engineers the highest-emotion sharing moment systematically

### Feature 6: Expansion Revenue Trigger
Trigger: Customer at 80% of plan limit → in-app notification with upgrade economics
Format: "You've used 87 of 100 documents. Upgrade to Pro for unlimited at $X/mo. Your current usage = $Y."
Logic: Customers hit limits and feel friction rather than a value proposition. This captures upgrade intent at peak motivation.

### Feature 7: Integration Depth Audit
What: Show customers which integrations they haven't activated that would reduce their friction
Format: "You're using HubSpot (connected) but not our Slack integration. Teams who connect Slack save 40% setup time."
Mechanism: Each additional integration activated = deeper switching cost moat

### Feature 8: Annual Contract Conversion Offer
Trigger: Day 30 after activation, if customer demonstrates genuine usage
Email: "[N] outputs generated. Lock in your rate for a year at 20% off."
Impact: Annual customers churn at 2-4x lower rates. Customer converting at Month 1 vs. canceling at Month 3 = $X vs. $4X total revenue.

### Feature 9: Competitor Pricing Intelligence Feed
What: Competitor pricing changes piped via webhook to internal Notion/Airtable dashboard
Response guide: Recommended action per change type
Advantage: 48-72 hour window after competitor price increase to capture their price-sensitive customers

### Feature 10: Customer Language Mining for Copy
Process: Weekly batch of support tickets + onboarding notes through LLM API
Prompt: "Extract exact phrases customers use to describe (a) the problem before using this product and (b) the value received."
Impact: Exact customer language on landing pages converts 15-30% better than founder-written copy
