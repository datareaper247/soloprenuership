# Brand Architecture and Naming

**Status:** Draft
**Last updated:** 2026-04-17
**Source IDs:** L01-L09, U01-U04, E01-E10

## Architecture decision

Use a **monobrand-first, founder-endorsed architecture**.

```text
Founder identity
└── Company identity: Eenmanzak / Eenmanszaak
    ├── Product/app identities only when earned
    │   ├── TapTap
    │   ├── SpatialSense
    │   └── Future ZZP/freelance tool
    ├── Service lines under company proof, not separate handles
    └── Support surfaces under company/product docs, not public handle sprawl
```

## Why this is right for the current stage

The company is still validating multiple lanes: a Dutch ZZP/freelance management concept, technical app pipeline, service/cashflow possibilities, and a founder-AI-operator thesis. Separate public brands for every lane would create premature complexity.

A monobrand-first setup gives the founder:

- one trust reservoir,
- one visual and naming system,
- one official company shell,
- one source of truth for proof,
- room to branch only when traction justifies it.

## Identity layers

### 1. Founder identity

**Role:** conviction, category design, technical credibility, journey, lessons, founder-led partnerships.

Use founder identity for:

- point of view on solo-founder leverage, Dutch solo business operations, and technical product building,
- build-in-public posts,
- lessons from TapTap / SpatialSense / service work,
- launch narratives,
- Product Hunt maker activity,
- relationship-building with potential users, partners, creators, and press.

Founder content should sound like a real person with judgment. Avoid hiding behind “we” too early.

### 2. Company identity

**Role:** official trust shell.

Use company identity for:

- official announcements,
- brand story and positioning,
- product roundup,
- customer proof/case studies,
- partnerships,
- hiring/collaboration asks,
- legal/contact/support links,
- media kit and press references,
- credibility router to products and founder.

Company voice should be calmer, shorter, and more structured than founder voice.

### 3. Product/app identities

**Role:** product-specific education, changelog, community, launch, support.

Create a product identity only when at least 3 of these are true:

- distinct ICP from the parent company,
- weekly or biweekly product updates,
- real support/community replies,
- launch plan beyond founder network,
- creator/partner/ad budget,
- future spin-out/sale potential,
- product name has independent search demand,
- product has enough proof to sustain a separate page.

If fewer than 3 are true, keep the product as a page on the company website, a GitHub repo/project, and a recurring content pillar under founder/company accounts.

### 4. Service identities

Do **not** create separate service socials at this stage.

Use:

- founder account for expertise,
- company account for proof/case studies,
- service pages on website,
- LinkedIn Service Page or Company Page sections where useful,
- private proposal templates and proof decks.

### 5. Support identity

Do **not** start with a public support handle.

Use:

- `support@...` inbox,
- website support page,
- product docs/FAQ,
- GitHub Issues only for developer/open-source projects,
- platform comments/DMs routed through a support playbook.

## Naming problem: `Eenmanzak` vs `Eenmanszaak`

The project registry uses `eenmanszaak` (the Dutch legal form). The user request says `eenmanzak`. Treat this as an unresolved naming decision.

### Option A — Use `Eenmanszaak`

**Pros**

- Immediately meaningful to Dutch audience.
- Strong SEO relevance for Dutch sole proprietorship topics.
- Aligns with existing registry and personal-finance context.

**Cons**

- Generic legal-form term, hard to own as a distinctive brand.
- Search results will compete with KVK, tax, bookkeeping, legal, and accounting content.
- Harder to trademark defensibly.
- Risk of sounding like a category page rather than a product/company.

### Option B — Use `Eenmanzak`

**Pros**

- More distinctive and ownable if intentional.
- Easier to reserve handles/domains if available.
- Can become a memorable brand if supported by clear explanation.

**Cons**

- Looks like a typo unless deliberately framed.
- May confuse Dutch speakers expecting `eenmanszaak`.
- Requires extra brand education.
- Must be checked for undesirable meanings/associations in Dutch.

### Recommended naming stance for now

Use this placeholder until founder review:

```text
Canonical legal/category spelling: Eenmanszaak
Stylized/defensive variant: Eenmanzak [use only after native-speaker and brand review]
Descriptor: operating system / tooling for Dutch one-person businesses and solo-founder products
Future product pattern: [Distinctive Product Name] by Eenmanszaak
```

Do not finalize public handles, domains, logos, or trademarks until:

1. Dutch-language native review confirms the name has no negative connotation.
2. Domain and handle availability are checked.
3. KVK/trademark/search feasibility is reviewed.
4. BOIP/trademark distinctiveness is considered for any product name.
5. The brand’s primary audience is clarified: Dutch ZZP'ers vs broader solo-founder product studio.

KVK-specific note: KVK distinguishes a **handelsnaam** (trade/company name in the Business Register) from a **merknaam** (brand/trademark registered through BOIP). KVK also states that one eenmanszaak may have multiple trade names and activities. That supports a cleaner architecture: keep the legal/category term accurate, but give customer-facing products distinctive names when they need trademark/search defensibility.

## Handle naming convention

Reserve in this order:

| Layer | Preferred pattern | Examples |
|---|---|---|
| Founder | real name | `ashwinsp`, `ashwinbuilds`, real-name variant |
| Company root | exact brand | `eenmanzak` |
| Company fallback | brand + suffix | `eenmanzakhq`, `useeenmanzak`, `getenmanzak`, `eenmanzak_io` |
| Product | exact product | `taptap`, `spatialsense` if available |
| Product fallback | product + app | `taptapapp`, `usespatialsense`, `spatialsenseapp` |
| Support | avoid public handle initially | use `support@` and help center |

Rules:

- Prefer the same handle across platforms.
- Avoid underscores unless platform forces them.
- Do not use legal suffixes (`bv`, `zzp`, `official`) unless required.
- Avoid “app” for the company root; use it only for actual apps.
- Reserve likely product handles privately, but do not publish separate accounts until earned.

## Brand voice

### Founder voice

Traits:

- precise,
- candid,
- builder-led,
- anti-hype,
- evidence-oriented,
- human and reflective.

Founder sentence pattern:

> “I learned X while building Y under constraint Z. Here is the practical implication.”

Avoid:

- generic hustle language,
- exaggerated AI claims,
- pretending the company is bigger than it is,
- legal/tax certainty where only adviser review can provide certainty.

### Company voice

Traits:

- calm,
- useful,
- trustworthy,
- operational,
- proof-first,
- concise.

Company sentence pattern:

> “We help [specific audience] achieve [specific outcome] without [specific pain].”

Until there is a team, it is acceptable for the company account to say “we” in official copy, but founder launch narratives should use “I” when describing founder work.

### Product voice

Traits:

- concrete,
- demo-led,
- user-outcome focused,
- support-aware,
- humble about roadmap.

Product sentence pattern:

> “Before: [pain]. After: [workflow/result]. Here is the demo.”

## Message hierarchy

### Company-level message pillars

1. **Solo-founder leverage** — systems and AI agents can multiply a focused individual without pretending constraints disappear.
2. **Dutch one-person business clarity** — run a cleaner, compliant, less chaotic solo operation.
3. **Proof through shipping** — demos, code, posts, customer language, and case studies beat abstract claims.
4. **Accessible technology** — products such as TapTap/SpatialSense show an empathy-led technical direction.
5. **Operational trust** — privacy, support, documentation, and governance are part of the product experience.

### Proof ladder

| Proof level | Asset | When to use |
|---|---|---|
| Level 1 | Founder story and constraints | Day 0 |
| Level 2 | Screenshots, demos, prototypes | Pre-launch |
| Level 3 | GitHub org/repos, changelog | Build phase |
| Level 4 | Waitlist/customer language | Validation |
| Level 5 | Testimonials/case studies | After permission |
| Level 6 | Revenue/user/retention metrics | Only when accurate and context-safe |

## Visual system draft principles

Detailed visual design is out of scope for this draft, but the direction should be:

- clear over clever,
- accessible over ornamental,
- Dutch/European restraint over Silicon Valley hype,
- product screenshots and diagrams over stock imagery,
- founder/operator notes over polished corporate abstraction.

Suggested visual motifs:

- grid / ledger / system map,
- one-person control room,
- calm operating dashboard,
- proof cards,
- before/after workflow screenshots,
- annotated product demos.

Accessibility guardrails:

- high contrast,
- readable type on mobile,
- captions on all video,
- no flashing thumbnails/GIFs,
- alt text for key graphics,
- avoid color-only status indicators.
