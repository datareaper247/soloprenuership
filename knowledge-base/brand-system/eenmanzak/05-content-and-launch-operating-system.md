# Content and Launch Operating System

**Status:** Draft
**Last updated:** 2026-04-17
**Source IDs:** U01-U06, L01-L09, E09-E17, E21-E25

## Content strategy in one sentence

Use founder-led content to create trust and research signals, company-led content to package proof, and product-led content only when product momentum creates a real audience.

## Stage calibration

Current context indicates validation/pre-launch. That means:

- prioritize direct customer learning over broad content scale,
- use content as market research, not vanity posting,
- choose one primary channel and one support channel,
- convert the best insights into durable assets,
- build launch assets before launch day.

## Website as the canonical content home

The company site is the durable home for the brand. Social channels discover and distribute; the website owns the current version of the story.

Use the site as the canonical source for:

- founder and company bios,
- product and service descriptions,
- proof assets and permissioned testimonials,
- contact and support routing,
- privacy, terms, and consent language,
- launch pages and evergreen explainer pages,
- updated links to the active social and proof surfaces.

Rules:

- if a claim matters, it should also exist on the site,
- if a CTA matters, it should have a canonical URL on the site,
- if a social post converts, the linked page should be the authoritative version of the offer,
- do not let a social post become the only record of an important claim.

## Primary audience hypotheses

There are two possible primary narratives. Pick one as the public lead; keep the other as a secondary pillar until evidence clarifies.

### Hypothesis A — Dutch ZZP / one-person business operator

**Audience:** Dutch self-employed professionals and solo business owners who need clearer operations, admin, and decision support.

Content angles:

- “What I wish every new Dutch sole proprietor understood before tools get messy.”
- “The one-person business operating checklist.”
- “From scattered admin to weekly operating rhythm.”
- “Practical, non-hype AI workflows for ZZP'ers.”

Best channels:

- LinkedIn,
- Reddit/community listening,
- website/templates,
- possibly Instagram/Pinterest for checklists.

### Hypothesis B — Solo technical founder product studio

**Audience:** solo technical founders, indie hackers, and technical operators building AI-era products.

Content angles:

- “Building products as a full-time employed solo founder in the Netherlands.”
- “AI-agent operating model for one-person companies.”
- “What I’m learning building TapTap / SpatialSense.”
- “Proof-first build logs: constraints, tradeoffs, decisions.”

Best channels:

- founder LinkedIn,
- GitHub,
- YouTube demos,
- Product Hunt,
- selective X/Bluesky later.

## Channel roles and cadence

### Founder LinkedIn — primary authority channel

Cadence:

- 3 posts/week.
- 10 thoughtful comments/week.
- 1 founder note/week saved as reusable content.

Formats:

1. **Observation post**
   - “I’ve noticed [specific pain] among [audience].”
   - “The hidden cost is [specific].”
   - “How are you solving this today?”

2. **Build log**
   - “This week I shipped/decided/killed [X].”
   - “Constraint: [Y]. Tradeoff: [Z].”

3. **Proof post**
   - demo screenshot,
   - architecture diagram,
   - before/after workflow,
   - customer language summary.

4. **Lesson post**
   - mistake,
   - correction,
   - principle.

### Company LinkedIn — official proof shell

Cadence:

- 1-2 posts/week.
- Monthly proof roundup.

Formats:

- “What changed this month.”
- “New resource: [template/checklist/demo].”
- “Product/update roundup.”
- “Customer/problem insight, anonymized.”
- “Partnership/collaboration ask.”

### YouTube — demo and explanation library

Cadence:

- Month 1: 1 company explainer + 2 short demos.
- Months 2-3: 1 deeper demo/tutorial per month.
- Cut each long video into 2-4 clips if Instagram/TikTok/Shorts are active.

Formats:

- product walkthrough,
- architecture explanation,
- “why this exists,”
- screen-recorded tutorial,
- launch video,
- founder decision breakdown.

Rules:

- captions on all videos,
- clear thumbnails,
- no synthetic realism without disclosure review,
- store source files with naming convention.

### GitHub — technical trust surface

Cadence:

- Public only when intentional.
- Monthly README/changelog update for public repos.

Formats:

- docs-first repos,
- small utilities,
- public roadmaps only where useful,
- security policy when public users exist.

### Reddit Pro — research engine

Cadence:

- 15 min/day listening.
- Weekly research synthesis.
- 3-5 value-first comments/week if relevant.

Weekly capture:

- exact phrases,
- objections,
- alternatives used today,
- communities where problem repeats,
- competitor/tool mentions,
- content ideas,
- product hypotheses.

Do not copy redditor content into public marketing without permission.

## First 90-day rollout

### Phase 0 — setup before posting heavily (days 0-14)

Outcome: trust shell exists.

Tasks:

- decide provisional public spelling/handle,
- reserve core handles,
- create business email aliases,
- create/optimize founder LinkedIn,
- create company LinkedIn Page,
- create GitHub org,
- create YouTube Brand Channel,
- create Product Hunt founder account,
- create Reddit Pro business profile,
- decide whether Instagram is active now or reserved only,
- publish minimum website pages and the canonical site router,
- draft privacy/terms/support pages,
- set up password manager + MFA + recovery vault,
- build access matrix,
- create processing register starter entries,
- create asset library.

Minimum website pages before serious posting:

1. Home with one-line value proposition and primary CTA.
2. About/founder page with the current founder story.
3. Proof page or proof section with demos and permissioned evidence.
4. Product/service page for the current public offer.
5. Contact page with the right inbox routing.
6. Support page with response expectations.
7. Privacy page and terms where needed.
8. Media/partnerships page or section if inbound matters.
9. Links page or footer that points back to the official profiles.

Content assets to create before regular publishing:

1. company one-liner,
2. founder one-liner,
3. category statement,
4. mission/positioning line,
5. 3-5 pillars,
6. 3 founder bio variants,
7. 3 company bio variants,
8. CTA library,
9. platform bios,

### Phase 1 — first 30 days

Outcome: public narrative is alive and coherent.

Weekly rhythm:

- Founder LinkedIn: 3 posts.
- Company LinkedIn: 1 proof/update post.
- Reddit Pro: 1 listening report.
- YouTube: 1 short/demo every 2 weeks.
- Website: 1 durable page/resource update.

First month themes:

1. Why the company exists.
2. What “one-person business as a system” means.
3. Current product/service portfolio and why focus matters.
4. Founder constraints and operating principles.
5. First audience research findings.
6. First demo/proof asset.

Success metrics:

- 10-20 meaningful comments/conversations,
- 5+ direct prospect/community conversations,
- 20+ waitlist/newsletter signups if form exists,
- 20 reusable content snippets captured,
- 3 strong customer-language insights,
- no stale/empty account surfaces.

### Phase 2 — days 31-60

Outcome: repeatable content format and research loop.

Focus:

- test 2 recurring founder formats,
- publish one long-form/company resource,
- start one product/service landing page,
- collect first testimonial/proof with permission,
- build Product Hunt draft if any product is nearing launch,
- decide whether product-specific account is warranted.

Metrics:

- engagement quality by pillar,
- profile click-through,
- website visits by UTM,
- waitlist conversion,
- number of direct conversations,
- questions repeated by prospects.

### Phase 3 — days 61-90

Outcome: launch-readiness and channel pruning.

Focus:

- prune or freeze underused channels,
- refine positioning based on real language,
- publish first flagship proof/resource,
- finalize launch assets for first product/service,
- introduce partner/creator shortlist only if message is working,
- consider Instagram/TikTok/Pinterest only if content engine exists.

Decision gates:

- If LinkedIn produces conversations: continue.
- If Reddit produces useful language but no public traction: keep as research, not posting channel.
- If Instagram/TikTok cannot publish 2+ useful posts/week: keep dormant/private, do not promote.
- If product has no support/content cadence: keep under company account.

## Launch system

Every product launch ships two things:

1. the product,
2. the story/proof package around the product.

### Minimum launch pack

- Product one-liner.
- Product Hunt tagline and 260-character description.
- Founder maker comment.
- LinkedIn founder launch post.
- Company launch post.
- YouTube demo.
- 3-5 screenshots/gallery images.
- FAQ/objection map.
- 20 warm outreach messages.
- 10 community replies/answers prepared.
- Support inbox ready.
- Privacy/terms ready.
- UTM links ready.
- Reply schedule for launch day.

### Product Hunt-specific reminders

- Post from personal maker account, not company account.
- Product Hunt day starts at 12:01 AM PST.
- Use direct product URL as primary link.
- Add YouTube full URL, not short/private video.
- Add first comment immediately.
- Respond to comments quickly during first hours.

## Content kill signals

- 30 days of posting with no meaningful conversations: reposition or change format.
- 60 days of broad content with no ICP clarity: narrow audience.
- Product posts get likes but no clicks/signups: improve CTA/landing page or audience fit.
- Comments show confusion about what company does: simplify one-liner and profile.
- Founder has to explain brand relationship repeatedly: account architecture is unclear.
