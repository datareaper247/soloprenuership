## 4. Strategic principles

1. **Proof before scale.**
   Every public claim should have a source, demo, user signal, shipped artifact, or explicit caveat.

2. **Founder-led, company-backed.**
   The founder carries voice and trust; the company carries official proof, policies, product routing, and support.

3. **One active public narrative at a time.**
   Keep the portfolio coherent by leading with one narrative and treating the others as supporting pillars until evidence changes.

4. **Compliance as trust, not theater.**
   Privacy, AI disclosure, platform rules, consumer-law, tax/WBSO, immigration/employment, and accessibility caveats are part of operating quality.

5. **Products earn independence.**
   A product gets its own account, campaign, or brand layer only when audience, cadence, support, and proof justify the overhead.

6. **AI is the operating layer.**
   AI should improve research, testing, documentation, support, content repurposing, and founder decision quality. It should not create fake certainty, fake scale, or unreviewed public output.

## 6. 90-day goal horizon — validation and repeatable operating cadence

**Primary objective:** turn the brand system into a weekly operating cadence that produces learning, proof, and audience signal.

### Goals

1. **Run one primary validation loop.**
   - Dutch ZZP: landing page + interviews/community listening + waitlist or early adopter calls.
   - TapTap: app-store readiness + demo/content proof + senior/adult-child learning signals.
   - Founder/product studio: proof-led LinkedIn/GitHub/YouTube cadence + service/collaboration inbound tests.

2. **Publish without overcommitting.**
   - Founder LinkedIn carries build notes, lessons, and questions.
   - Company shell publishes proof roundups, resources, and official links.
   - Product pages/accounts are created only if launch/support cadence exists.

3. **Measure learning, not vanity.**
   Track:
   - qualified conversations,
   - waitlist or demo interest,
   - repeated pain language,
   - replies/comments from target audience,
   - proof assets produced,
   - support/privacy/compliance gaps found before launch.

4. **Codify a weekly company review.**
   - What shipped?
   - What was learned from audience/customer language?
   - Which claim needs evidence or deletion?
   - Which product/service lane deserves next-week focus?
   - Which channel/account should be pruned, paused, or left dormant?

5. **Convert the company story into public assets.**
   - Founder About section.
   - Company About page.
   - Portfolio page.
   - Product one-pagers for active products only.
   - Launch checklist for whichever product/service becomes first public focus.

### 90-day acceptance evidence

- 6-10 qualified audience conversations or equivalent strong market-learning events are logged.
- 4 weekly review notes exist with decisions, not just activity summaries.
- At least one product/service lane has a clear continue/kill/pause decision.
- Public profiles and website copy are consistent with the claims register.
- No more than two public channels require active weekly maintenance unless they are demonstrably useful.

## 7. 365-day goal horizon — one credible business engine

**Primary objective:** by one year, the company should have one credible revenue/traction engine and a cleaner portfolio logic.

### Goals

1. **One lane produces material evidence.**
   Evidence could be revenue, paying pilots, App Store traction, qualified B2B pipeline, repeatable service revenue, or a clearly validated waitlist with conversion intent. The exact metric needs founder selection.

2. **Portfolio is simplified.**
   Each lane is classified as:
   - active business engine,
   - strategic option,
   - proof/credibility asset,
   - parked idea,
   - killed idea.

3. **Trust infrastructure is mature enough for scale.**
   - Processing register maintained.
   - Access review cadence running.
   - Permission log for testimonials/proof assets.
   - Product-specific risk reviews before stronger claims.
   - Website remains canonical source.

4. **Founder/company narrative is durable.**
   The public story should make immediate sense without explaining every experiment: a senior engineer building practical products for solo operators and underserved users, using AI and disciplined operations to compound output.

5. **Launch playbook exists.**
   The company can repeatedly launch products/resources with a known checklist for claims, copy, channels, privacy, support, Product Hunt/YouTube/GitHub, and post-launch learning.

### 365-day acceptance evidence

- One primary business engine is selected using evidence, not founder excitement alone.
- Revenue/traction target is quantified by founder review.
- The brand system has been pruned at least twice based on channel and product evidence.
- Public claims have not outgrown support, privacy, legal, accessibility, or product proof.
- The founder can explain the company in one sentence, one paragraph, and one portfolio map.

## 8. 3-year goal horizon — compounding solo company

**Primary objective:** become a resilient, founder-led product company with a small number of trusted assets instead of a graveyard of experiments.

### Goals

1. **Operate as a trusted solo-company platform.**
   The company has repeatable systems for discovery, shipping, support, compliance, content, and launch — lightweight enough for a solo founder, serious enough for customers and partners.

2. **Maintain a focused product portfolio.**
   Ideally 1-3 public products/services have independent proof. Anything else remains internal tooling, research, or archived learning.

3. **Own a defensible narrative.**
   Possible narratives:
   - Dutch one-person business operating system.
   - Accessibility-first practical product studio.
   - AI-enabled solo-operator company.
   - Senior-engineer product lab for overlooked everyday workflows.

4. **Turn SoloOS-style operations into leverage.**
   Internal agentic workflows become a real operating advantage: faster research, safer launches, better documentation, sharper decisions, and lower cognitive load.

5. **Keep founder trust intact.**
   The founder remains credible because public output stayed honest: no fake scale, no unverified compliance promises, no abandoned channel sprawl, and no overclaimed product safety.

### 3-year acceptance evidence

- A small, coherent portfolio has measurable revenue, user, or partnership proof.
- Founder/company/product identities are still consistent and maintained.
- At least one product has earned independent brand/support surface by the criteria in `02-brand-architecture-and-naming.md`.
- The company can withstand due diligence: source-backed claims, permissioned proof, documented privacy/support processes, and clear ownership of accounts/assets.
- The founder has a clear next-stage choice: lifestyle cashflow, product portfolio, acquisition-ready asset, or fundable company path.

## 10. Caveats and do-not-overclaim list

- Do not state that the company is registered, trademarked, WBSO-approved, tax-optimized, GDPR-compliant, AI-Act-compliant, EAA-compliant, or legally safe unless current evidence and professional review support it.
- Do not imply employer endorsement or full-time founder status if not accurate.
- Do not use TapTap or SpatialSense claims that imply guaranteed outcomes, medical/safety reliability, or replacement of professional/assistive support.
- Do not publish testimonials, user quotes, screenshots, or logos without permission records.
- Do not let `AI-enabled` copy imply autonomous decisions, autonomous posting, or unreviewed professional advice.
- Do not create a 3-year public promise until the founder has selected a business model and critical-path metric.
