## Working portfolio thesis

The cleanest portfolio logic is not “one company with many random ideas.” It is:

> **A founder-led software company that compounds trust by shipping practical operator tools: accessibility-first apps, Dutch one-person-business workflows, and AI-assisted internal operating systems.**

That gives the portfolio one coherent reason to exist while preserving optionality:

- **TapTap** proves patient, accessibility-aware product craft for non-technical users.
- **SpatialSense** extends the accessibility thread into experimental spatial-awareness tooling, but must stay safety-caveated.
- **Dutch ZZP / one-person-business tooling** turns founder-local operating pain into a market-facing workflow product.
- **Services / consulting** provide controlled cashflow and customer discovery without becoming a separate brand.
- **SoloOS / AI operator infrastructure** is the internal leverage system and possible future thought-leadership/product thread, not the first thing to sell broadly unless demand becomes explicit.

## Recommended sequencing hypothesis

### Phase 0 — Align the story before adding surfaces

**Goal:** prevent fragmented copy across TapTap, SpatialSense, ZZP tooling, services, and SoloOS.

1. Choose the public lead for the next 30-60 days:
   - **Product-proof lead:** TapTap is the first public proof asset.
   - **Market-problem lead:** Dutch ZZP workflow is the first audience research / waitlist asset.
   - **Founder-operator lead:** SoloOS/AI operator method is the thought-leadership wrapper.
2. Keep all non-lead lanes as supporting pillars, not equal public CTAs.
3. Use company site as the canonical router: `/products/taptap`, `/research/zzp-workflows`, `/notes/ai-operator`, `/services` or similar.

**Acceptance criteria for this phase:** one homepage hero can explain the portfolio without listing five disconnected projects.

### Phase 1 — Ship one proof lane while researching one market lane

**Recommended default:**

- **TapTap = proof of product craft.** Use demos, accessibility-oriented UX decisions, and launch preparation as concrete evidence.
- **Dutch ZZP product = market research lane.** Use listening, interviews, templates/checklists, and waitlist experiments before committing product architecture.
- **Services = selective cashflow / discovery.** Package services only where they reinforce the product thesis, such as AI-assisted workflow audits, automation prototypes, or accessible product engineering.
- **SoloOS = operating-method content.** Share founder lessons, not a sales promise.
- **SpatialSense = R&D footnote.** Keep it inspiring but cautious until prototype/safety review matures.

**Why this sequence:** TapTap already has build evidence, while the Dutch ZZP opportunity needs customer language and compliance-safe scoping. Running one proof lane plus one research lane is feasible for a solo founder; trying to publicly launch every lane at once will look scattered.

### Phase 2 — Let traction decide product independence

Use the existing product-account decision tree, but apply it to product strategy too:

- 0-2 positive signals: keep as company content / private experiment.
- 3-4 positive signals: create a landing page, demo playlist, waitlist, or docs hub.
- 5-6 positive signals: create a distinct product identity, dedicated roadmap, and support workflow.

Signals to count:

1. distinct ICP repeats the problem in their own language,
2. weekly/biweekly updates are available,
3. real support or community replies are needed,
4. launch goes beyond founder network,
5. paid acquisition/creator/partner motion is planned,
6. the product has spin-out/search/trademark value.

## Product-by-product logic

### TapTap

**Strategic job:** show that the founder can ship careful, human-centered software for a high-trust audience.

**Public narrative angle:**

> “Accessible technology is not a slogan. TapTap is where I learned how hard it is to make mobile workflows patient, visual, and confidence-building for older adults.”

**Use TapTap to prove:**

- patience and empathy in product design,
- accessibility-aware UX judgment,
- operational seriousness around privacy, terms, support, and App Store readiness,
- ability to turn a narrow user problem into a polished product surface.

**Do not overclaim:**

- guaranteed learning outcomes,
- clinical/accessibility certification,
- Apple affiliation or endorsement,
- launch status before live URLs, privacy, ToS, and App Store state are true.

**Next-step hypotheses:**

- Make TapTap the first product demo in founder/company proof assets if legal/privacy/IP review is complete.
- Keep TapTap under company/founder accounts initially; separate product socials are unnecessary until launch/support cadence exists.
- Create one canonical TapTap proof page with screenshots, demo video, support/privacy links, and status wording.

### SpatialSense

**Strategic job:** signal a deeper accessibility R&D direction without creating unsafe claims.

**Public narrative angle:**

> “SpatialSense is an experimental exploration of mobile sensors, audio, and spatial awareness — an R&D thread, not a safety promise.”

**Use SpatialSense to prove:**

- technical ambition beyond simple CRUD apps,
- continuity with accessibility-first values,
- founder capacity for sensor/on-device/mobile experimentation.

**Do not overclaim:**

- safe navigation,
- medical/assistive-device equivalence,
- replacement for cane, guide dog, professional mobility training, or situational awareness,
- launch timeline before prototype and review.

**Next-step hypotheses:**

- Keep SpatialSense as a “future R&D / accessibility experiments” line in portfolio copy.
- Do not make it the first public CTA.
- Use it in founder content only when paired with explicit caveats from the claims register.

### Dutch ZZP / one-person-business product

**Strategic job:** become the highest-fit commercial workflow product if audience and compliance-safe scope validate.

**Public narrative angle:**

> “A calmer operating layer for Dutch one-person businesses: weekly rhythm, source-linked workflows, and clearer admin decisions — without pretending software replaces qualified advisers.”

**Use this lane to prove:**

- founder-market fit as a Netherlands-based solo operator,
- practical customer research,
- ability to simplify confusing workflows,
- responsible boundary-setting around legal/tax/accounting/immigration claims.

**Do not overclaim:**

- “guaranteed compliant,”
- “no accountant needed,”
- “tax savings,”
- definitive KVK/VAT/WBSO/immigration advice,
- official government endorsement.

**Next-step hypotheses:**

- Validate problem language before building: interviews, Reddit/listening, LinkedIn posts, waitlist, downloadable checklist.
- Scope MVP around workflow clarity and document/rhythm support, not authoritative advice.
- Treat the name decision (`Eenmanszaak` vs `Eenmanzak`) as a blocker for public product branding, but not for private research.

### Services / cashflow

**Strategic job:** fund learning and generate proof without creating brand sprawl.

**Public narrative angle:**

> “Selective services help turn real operating problems into shipped systems and reusable product insight.”

**Best-fit service packages as hypotheses:**

1. **AI-assisted workflow audit** for solo operators or small teams.
2. **Automation prototype sprint** for a specific operational bottleneck.
3. **Accessibility-aware mobile/product review** drawing from TapTap/SpatialSense lessons.
4. **Founder operating-system setup** as a private/internalized version of SoloOS practices, if demand appears.

**Keep services under:** founder expertise + company proof + case studies. Do not create separate service brands or accounts.

**Next-step hypotheses:**

- Offer 1-2 tightly scoped service packages only if they strengthen product discovery.
- Capture permissioned before/after case evidence.
- Kill or pause any service that consumes product-build capacity without generating reusable insight or cashflow.

### SoloOS / AI operator thread

**Strategic job:** explain why the founder can run a serious portfolio without pretending to have a large team.

**Public narrative angle:**

> “I use AI agents as an operating layer for research, planning, QA, and execution discipline — with human judgment and review at the steering wheel.”

**Use SoloOS to prove:**

- systematic execution,
- quality and risk control,
- repeatable decision-making,
- founder leverage under full-time-job and family constraints.

**Do not overclaim:**

- fully autonomous business operations,
- unsupervised legal/tax/privacy decisions,
- AI replacing founder accountability,
- customer data processing without privacy review.

**Next-step hypotheses:**

- Use SoloOS as content/perspective before productizing it.
- Publicize process artifacts selectively: decision records, kill signals, launch checklists, review loops.
- Productize only if external users ask for the operating system itself, not merely the products it helps ship.

## Portfolio coherence options

### Option A — Accessibility-first product studio

**Lead:** TapTap now, SpatialSense later.

**One-liner hypothesis:**

> “Building patient, accessibility-aware mobile products that help people use technology with more confidence.”

**Best when:** TapTap is closest to launch and proof assets are ready.

**Risk:** Dutch ZZP product and SoloOS may feel bolted on unless framed as operating infrastructure.

### Option B — Dutch solo-operator operating system

**Lead:** ZZP workflows and one-person-business clarity.

**One-liner hypothesis:**

> “Helping Dutch one-person businesses run calmer operations with practical software, checklists, and AI-assisted workflows.”

**Best when:** the founder wants the company brand to own the Dutch local market.

**Risk:** TapTap/SpatialSense become side projects unless explained as proof of product craft and accessibility values.

### Option C — Founder-led practical software portfolio

**Lead:** founder/company trust; products are proof points.

**One-liner hypothesis:**

> “A founder-led software company building practical tools for solo operators, accessible mobile workflows, and AI-assisted execution.”

**Best when:** optionality matters and no single product should dominate yet.

**Risk:** too broad unless the homepage chooses one primary CTA.

### Recommended default for review

Use **Option C** as the parent narrative for the next workshop, but force a **single primary CTA** for the next 30-60 days. The likely default CTA is either:

1. “Watch / join TapTap launch updates” if product proof is ready, or
2. “Join the Dutch solo-business workflow research list” if the ZZP lane is the commercial priority.

## Portfolio kill signals

| Risk | Kill / pause signal | Response |
|---|---|---|
| Portfolio sprawl | More than two lanes ask for public content in the same week | Pick one lead lane and one support lane; archive the rest into monthly roundup only |
| Service drag | Services consume product-build time without cashflow or reusable insight | Pause services or narrow to one product-adjacent package |
| Unsafe claims | Draft copy implies legal/tax/safety/accessibility certainty | Route through claims register; replace with conservative wording |
| Product identity sprawl | Dedicated product account has no weekly updates/support need | Keep product under company/founder surfaces |
| SoloOS overexposure | Audience engages with tooling meta-content but not product/customer problems | Re-anchor to user outcomes and proof assets |
| ZZP ambiguity | Audience asks adviser-style questions the product cannot safely answer | Re-scope to workflow support and adviser handoff |
