## Company mandate alignment

Interpret this document through `00-company-mandate-and-alignment.md`: the company is an AI-first founder-led software company using senior software engineering and ecommerce/logistics expertise to build B2B/B2C apps, SaaS/micro-SaaS, consulting, R&D, OSS/content authority, and multiple revenue streams. Dutch ZZP, TapTap, SpatialSense, and SoloOS are lanes, not the entire company identity.

## Core audience problem

The current brand system is serving two overlapping but different markets:

1. **Dutch one-person business / ZZP operator** — needs calmer admin, operational clarity, practical AI/tooling, and trust-sensitive guidance.
2. **Solo technical founder / product operator** — needs proof-first execution systems, AI-agent workflows, portfolio clarity, demos, launch discipline, and founder-led trust.

The risk is trying to speak to both with equal priority from day one. The opportunity is to use the founder story as the bridge while sequencing public CTAs so each audience sees a clear next step.

## Recommended sequencing hypothesis

### Sequencing principle

Lead with the audience that has the most urgent pain, clearest trust requirement, and easiest research path **right now**, while keeping the other as a visible secondary pillar.

Recommended default sequence for validation:

1. **Research wedge:** Dutch ZZP / one-person business operators.
2. **Trust wedge:** founder as senior engineer building seriously from the Netherlands.
3. **Proof wedge:** small portfolio of practical apps, services, and operating-system assets.
4. **Expansion wedge:** solo technical founders / AI-enabled operators once proof assets exist.

Rationale: the Dutch ZZP lane has a concrete term, immediate admin/business-operation pains, and strong need for conservative trust language. The solo-founder lane is strategically valuable, but more meta and easier to over-index into build-in-public content before there is user proof.

## ICP hypotheses

### ICP 1 — New or overwhelmed Dutch ZZP'er / one-person business owner

**Draft hypothesis:** A solo business owner in the Netherlands who is competent at their craft but feels operationally scattered across admin, decisions, tools, compliance reminders, content, client pipeline, and support.

**Likely pains**

- “I know I need a business rhythm, but I do not know what to check weekly.”
- “I am using too many tools and still miss important follow-ups.”
- “I want AI help, but I do not trust generic advice for Dutch context.”
- “I need plain-language workflows, not another accounting/legal promise.”
- “I want to look professional without becoming a full company bureaucracy.”

**Desired outcomes**

- calmer weekly operating rhythm,
- fewer scattered tools and notes,
- clearer next actions,
- conservative reminders for adviser-sensitive topics,
- confidence that public/company basics are not chaotic.

**Best early offer / CTA hypotheses**

- “One-person business operating checklist.”
- “Weekly ZZP operating rhythm template.”
- “Privacy/support/social setup checklist for a small public business.”
- “AI-assisted admin triage workflow, with adviser-review gates.”
- Lightweight waitlist for a future Dutch one-person-business operating tool.

**Proof needed before stronger claims**

- screenshots or demo of the workflow,
- founder’s own operating-system example,
- anonymized research synthesis,
- clear caveats around legal/tax/privacy/accounting,
- privacy/support pages before collecting leads.

### ICP 2 — Solo technical founder / indie product operator

**Draft hypothesis:** A technical founder or builder who wants to execute a portfolio of apps/services with AI leverage, but needs better prioritization, operating cadence, public proof, and launch discipline.

**Likely pains**

- “I can build many things, but I need a system that compounds instead of fragments.”
- “I need AI agents as leverage, not noisy automation.”
- “My product portfolio story is unclear.”
- “I want to ship with proof, not hype.”
- “I need a serious company shell even if I am solo.”

**Desired outcomes**

- clean portfolio logic,
- AI-assisted execution cadence,
- proof-first launch assets,
- better public narrative,
- reduced fragmentation across products and accounts.

**Best early offer / CTA hypotheses**

- “Solo founder operating system notes.”
- “AI-agent founder workflow breakdown.”
- “Portfolio decision log: what to ship, pause, or kill.”
- “Founder-led brand operating system template.”
- YouTube/GitHub demos around TapTap, SpatialSense, or internal SoloOS patterns.

**Proof needed before stronger claims**

- shipped artifacts,
- clear before/after workflows,
- decision records,
- public repos or demo videos where safe,
- evidence that AI workflows are human-reviewed and not autonomous public action.

### ICP 3 — Accessibility-adjacent caregivers, trainers, and senior-support networks

**Draft hypothesis:** People around older adults or accessibility-sensitive users who care about digital confidence and practical mobile support, but are not necessarily buyers of a broad company operating system.

**Likely pains**

- “Older users need simple, patient guidance.”
- “Smartphone learning is stressful when instructions assume too much.”
- “Assistive concepts must be careful and safety-aware.”

**Best early offer / CTA hypotheses**

- TapTap demo/watchlist,
- senior-friendly smartphone learning resources,
- accessibility-first design notes,
- partnerships or user-research calls.

**Position in sequence**

Keep as a **product/proof audience**, not the first company-wide ICP, unless TapTap or SpatialSense becomes the first launch object.

### ICP 4 — Service / consulting buyers

**Draft hypothesis:** Small companies or founders who want the founder’s senior engineering / AI-operator capability as service capacity.

**Likely pains**

- “I need a senior technical partner who can think product and execution.”
- “I want AI-enabled workflows without losing control.”
- “I need pragmatic software, not a big-agency process.”

**Position in sequence**

Use services as a **cashflow and proof lane**, not a separate public audience. The founder profile and company proof page can carry this without creating separate service branding.

## Anti-ICP hypotheses

Avoid optimizing for these audiences early:

1. **People seeking guaranteed legal/tax/accounting answers**
   - Risk: impossible and unsafe without professional adviser scope.
   - Safe redirect: general workflow support plus adviser-review gates.

2. **High-volume content hackers / growth-at-all-costs audiences**
   - Risk: clashes with trust-first, conservative public posture.
   - Safe redirect: proof-first founder content and useful templates.

3. **Large enterprises needing procurement, SLAs, and multi-seat governance now**
   - Risk: too much support burden for a solo operator.
   - Safe redirect: future partnership/service discussion only when capacity exists.

4. **Users expecting fully autonomous AI handling of public posting, legal decisions, accounting, support, or DMs**
   - Risk: conflicts with human-review guardrails and compliance register.
   - Safe redirect: human-approved AI assistance.

5. **Consumers who need regulated financial/legal/tax advice**
   - Risk: adviser boundary and claim risk.
   - Safe redirect: “general information only; verify with a qualified professional.”

6. **Product users who require safety-critical navigation or medical/assistive-device guarantees**
   - Risk: SpatialSense-style liability and medical-device claims.
   - Safe redirect: experimental assistive spatial-awareness concept with explicit non-replacement caveats.

7. **Audience segments that demand always-on public support through social DMs**
   - Risk: privacy/support overload.
   - Safe redirect: support inbox, support page, response expectations.

## Trust barriers by audience

### Dutch ZZP / one-person business operator

**Barriers**

- “Is this actually valid for Dutch rules?”
- “Is this a bookkeeping/legal/tax service pretending to be software?”
- “Will my data be handled carefully?”
- “Is this founder/company real and accountable?”
- “Is AI making claims it should not make?”

**Trust assets needed**

- clear caveat library,
- privacy statement before lead capture,
- processing-register starter,
- support/contact page,
- conservative wording for legal/tax/privacy,
- founder/company identity clarity,
- explicit adviser-review boundaries.

### Solo technical founder / AI operator

**Barriers**

- “Is this just another AI-agent hype thread?”
- “Has the founder shipped anything real?”
- “Can I see the actual system or demos?”
- “Is the portfolio coherent?”

**Trust assets needed**

- demos, changelogs, screenshots,
- transparent decision records,
- GitHub/YouTube proof where safe,
- honest constraints,
- examples of human-in-the-loop AI usage.

### Accessibility / senior-support network

**Barriers**

- “Is this respectful and usable for older adults?”
- “Is this making unsafe assistive claims?”
- “Was it tested with real users?”

**Trust assets needed**

- careful product caveats,
- accessibility-oriented design notes,
- non-medical/non-safety-critical wording,
- permissioned user research once available,
- captions and accessible demo content.

### Service / consulting buyer

**Barriers**

- “Is the founder available and reliable?”
- “Is the company setup serious enough?”
- “Can this person handle production constraints?”

**Trust assets needed**

- founder senior-engineering credibility,
- clear service boundaries,
- case-study/proof format,
- business contact route,
- no contradiction between employer identity and founder side-business wording.

## Message hierarchy by sequence

### If Dutch ZZP is primary

**Public lead:**

> Draft hypothesis: practical software and operating-system workflows for Dutch one-person businesses that want calmer operations, clearer decisions, and conservative AI assistance.

**Secondary proof:** founder is a senior software engineer building and documenting the system in public.

**Avoid:** implying legal/tax/accounting compliance outcomes.

### If solo technical founders are primary

**Public lead:**

> Draft hypothesis: a founder-led product studio showing how a solo technical operator uses software, AI agents, and proof-first systems to build useful products under real constraints.

**Secondary proof:** Dutch one-person-business tooling is the first domain/product wedge.

**Avoid:** becoming too meta before product/user proof exists.

### If accessibility product becomes primary

**Public lead:**

> Draft hypothesis: practical, respectful mobile-learning and assistive workflow products for accessibility-sensitive users and their support networks.

**Secondary proof:** the founder/company operating system shows serious governance and product discipline.

**Avoid:** safety-critical or medical-style claims.

## Validation plan

### Research questions

1. Which audience has the strongest “this is painful now” language: Dutch ZZP operators or solo technical founders?
2. What do Dutch ZZP operators already use for weekly business control: accountant, spreadsheet, bookkeeping tool, Notion, calendar, WhatsApp, memory?
3. Which trust barrier blocks conversion first: legal/tax fear, privacy/data concern, unknown founder/company, or unclear product outcome?
4. Which CTA gets more qualified interest: checklist/template, demo, waitlist, or interview call?
5. Does `Eenmanzak` read as clever, confusing, or wrong to native Dutch speakers?
6. Which proof asset most increases confidence: founder credentials, demo, source-linked checklist, privacy/support page, or customer language?

### Lightweight tests

| Test | Audience | Asset | Success signal |
|---|---|---|---|
| LinkedIn question post | Dutch ZZP + founder network | “What is the weekly business check you wish existed?” | concrete replies/DMs from target users |

## Suggested 30 / 90 / 365-day audience milestones

### 30 days — audience discovery and trust shell

- Choose one primary audience hypothesis for public lead.
- Collect 25-50 raw audience phrases from LinkedIn/community/research calls.
- Publish a conservative landing page with caveats, support/contact, and privacy basics before lead capture.
- Test one low-risk CTA.
- Decide what not to pursue publicly yet.

### 90 days — validated wedge and first offer clarity

- Identify the top 2-3 repeated pains and current alternatives.
- Have one proof asset that can be reused across founder/company/product surfaces.
- Turn audience language into a clearer one-liner and homepage path.
- Decide whether the next public push is Dutch ZZP tool, founder operating system content, accessibility product proof, or services.

### 365 days — segment expansion or focus

- Either double down on the strongest audience wedge or split product identity only if a distinct ICP has earned it.
- Maintain one canonical company proof hub with segment-specific pages.
- Build a permissioned proof library: testimonials, case studies, demos, changelogs, source-linked resources.
- Revisit anti-ICP and support boundaries before scaling content or accounts.

## Safe copy snippets for audience testing

Use these only as draft starting points:

- “I am exploring practical software and workflows for Dutch one-person businesses that want calmer operations and clearer weekly decisions. I am not trying to replace legal, tax, or accounting advice.”
- “I am collecting the real language of solo operators: where admin, tools, decisions, and AI help feel useful — and where they feel risky.”
- “The goal is not autonomous AI running your business. The goal is better prompts, checklists, reminders, and review gates so a solo operator stays in control.”
- “For solo technical founders, I am documenting the operating system behind building useful products under constraint: what to ship, what to pause, and how to keep proof close to the product.”
- “For accessibility-focused product work, I am keeping claims careful: demos and learning support first, no safety-critical promises.”

## Caveats to preserve

- Do not promise legal, tax, accounting, immigration, privacy, accessibility, medical, or advertising compliance.
- Do not claim KVK/VAT/WBSO/trademark/verification status without current evidence.
- Do not collect leads/newsletter/support data without privacy notice and explicit purpose.
- Do not use testimonials, screenshots, names, logos, or customer quotes without documented permission.
- Do not imply AI autonomously posts, comments, DMs, advises, or makes regulated decisions.
- Do not make safety-critical claims for SpatialSense-style concepts.
