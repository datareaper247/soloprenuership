## ADR-001 — Use a 3-layer identity model

**Decision:** Adopt founder + company + selective product/app identity layers.

**Rationale:** The company needs founder trust, official company credibility, and product-specific surfaces only when traction justifies them. This prevents handle sprawl and aligns with platform mechanics.

**Rejected:** Create separate accounts for every product/service idea.
**Reason:** Too much maintenance, high risk of abandoned accounts, diluted trust.

**Status:** Recommended.

## ADR-002 — Default to monobrand first

**Decision:** Use one company brand and one visual/message system until a product earns independence.

**Rationale:** Current context includes multiple possible lanes. Monobrand lets proof compound under one shell while preserving optionality.

**Rejected:** House-of-brands from day one.
**Reason:** Premature for validation/pre-launch; requires more content/support capacity than a solo founder has.

**Status:** Recommended.

## ADR-003 — Treat `Eenmanzak` vs `Eenmanszaak` as an unresolved naming decision

**Decision:** Use `Eenmanszaak` for legal/category/trust-sensitive documentation now; reserve `Eenmanzak` defensively or as a stylized alias only after Dutch native-speaker and brand-ownability review. Do not finalize public brand assets until spelling and ownability are reviewed.

**Rationale:** `Eenmanszaak` is the Dutch legal form and appears in project context; `Eenmanzak` appears in the user request and may be the intended brand. The difference materially affects distinctiveness, SEO, handles, and trust.

**Next validation:** Dutch native-language review, domain/handle search, KVK/trademark/search feasibility, audience comprehension test.

**Status:** Recommended default: `Eenmanszaak` for trust-sensitive surfaces; public stylized name remains open.

## ADR-004 — Create founder LinkedIn and company LinkedIn now

**Decision:** Use LinkedIn as the primary B2B/professional trust channel.

**Rationale:** Platform supports personal profile + company Page + Product/Service/Showcase structure. Founder authority and company official trust are both needed.

**Status:** Recommended.

## ADR-005 — Create GitHub org and YouTube Brand Channel now

**Decision:** Create technical proof and demo surfaces early.

**Rationale:** GitHub org supports product/code trust. YouTube Brand Channel supports demos, explainers, Product Hunt galleries, and future tutorials.

**Status:** Recommended.

## ADR-006 — Use Reddit Pro as research before promotion

**Decision:** Use Reddit Pro for listening, keyword monitoring, community discovery, and customer language mining before posting product promotions.

**Rationale:** Reddit is high-signal for language and objections but punishes inauthentic promotion. Reddit Pro source terms also caution against publishing tool data or redditor content without consent.

**Status:** Recommended.

## ADR-007 — Defer TikTok and Pinterest unless cadence exists

**Decision:** Do not create active TikTok/Pinterest unless the company can sustain native short-form/visual content.

**Rationale:** Inactive TikTok accounts may lose usernames after 180 days; Pinterest requires visual/search asset consistency. Both can distract from the primary LinkedIn/website/research loop.

**Status:** Recommended.

## ADR-008 — No public support handle initially

**Decision:** Use support inbox, support page, docs, Product Hunt/GitHub issue surfaces as appropriate; do not create `@brand_support` early.

**Rationale:** A public support handle implies staffed support and constant monitoring. A solo founder needs controlled routing first.

**Status:** Recommended.

## ADR-009 — Human approval for all public AI-assisted output

**Decision:** AI may help produce content, but humans approve public posts, replies, DMs, videos, ads, and creator briefs.

**Rationale:** Platform rules and trust risk require accountability. LinkedIn says posters remain responsible; TikTok/YouTube require disclosures for some AI/synthetic content.

**Status:** Recommended.
