# Low-Liability Consulting SOW Template and Prohibited-Project Checklist

**Status:** Draft template; lawyer/accountant/insurance review required before use
**Last updated:** 2026-04-17
**Applies to:** Consulting, productized services, automation prototypes, audits, and implementation support sold under the company/founder brand.


## Company mandate alignment

Interpret this document through `00-company-mandate-and-alignment.md`: the company is an AI-first founder-led software company using senior software engineering and ecommerce/logistics expertise to build B2B/B2C apps, SaaS/micro-SaaS, consulting, R&D, OSS/content authority, and multiple revenue streams. Dutch ZZP, TapTap, SpatialSense, and SoloOS are lanes, not the entire company identity.

> This is not a legal contract. It is a structured checklist and drafting scaffold to discuss with a Dutch lawyer/insurance adviser before accepting paid consulting work.

## 1. Consulting strategy

Consulting should be used only if it supports at least one of:

- cashflow;
- customer discovery;
- proof/case-study creation;
- product insight;
- repeatable service-to-product learning;
- founder/company trust.

Do not create a separate consulting brand at this stage. Use founder/company trust shell and keep service scope narrow.

## 2. Default low-liability offers

Good early offers:

1. **AI-assisted workflow audit**
   - output: workflow map, automation opportunities, risk notes;
   - not legal/tax/compliance advice.

2. **Automation prototype sprint**
   - output: working prototype or proof of concept;
   - no uptime/SLA/mission-critical guarantees.

3. **Product trust-shell setup**
   - output: official-site pages, support/privacy routing, account map, launch checklist;
   - claims-register based.

4. **App-store readiness checklist support**
   - output: submission checklist, privacy/support/metadata review notes;
   - not legal/IP/app-store approval guarantee.

5. **Documentation/ops system setup**
   - output: SOPs, registers, source index, decision records;
   - client owns final legal/compliance validation.

Avoid offering “done-for-you compliance” or regulated professional conclusions.

## 3. Prohibited-project checklist

Reject or escalate before accepting if any are true:

- [ ] Client wants legal advice.
- [ ] Client wants tax/accounting advice.
- [ ] Client wants immigration advice.
- [ ] Client wants medical/health/safety claims.
- [ ] Client wants cybersecurity guarantee or breach indemnity.
- [ ] Project is emergency, navigation, medical, financial, legal, or safety-critical.
- [ ] Contract requires uncapped liability.
- [ ] Contract requires personal guarantee.
- [ ] Contract requires broad indemnity for third-party claims.
- [ ] Client wants use of sensitive/special-category data without DPA/security review.
- [ ] Client wants automated decisions affecting legal, financial, health, employment, housing, education, or credit outcomes.
- [ ] Client asks to bypass platform, tax, privacy, accessibility, or advertising rules.
- [ ] Client demands public claims you cannot verify.
- [ ] Client is in a sanctioned/high-risk jurisdiction or the project has unclear export/sanctions risk.

If any box is checked, do not proceed without legal + insurance + entity review.

## 4. SOW skeleton

```text
Statement of Work — [Project Name]

1. Parties
- Client: [legal name, address, registration number]
- Service provider: [legal/trade name, address, KVK number when available]

2. Background
- Client wants: [business problem]
- Provider will deliver: [specific service]
- This SOW does not include: [explicit exclusions]

3. Scope
Included:
- [deliverable 1]
- [deliverable 2]
- [deliverable 3]

Excluded:
- legal, tax, accounting, immigration, medical, financial, investment, or regulated advice;
- production operation unless explicitly included;
- security guarantee;
- platform approval guarantee;
- compliance certification;
- emergency/safety-critical use.

4. Deliverables
| Deliverable | Format | Acceptance criteria | Due date |
|---|---|---|---|
| [name] | [doc/prototype/call] | [specific criteria] | [date] |

5. Client responsibilities
- provide accurate information;
- provide access on time;
- review deliverables within [x] business days;
- obtain professional legal/tax/privacy/security review where needed;
- do not use prototype outputs for regulated decisions without review.

6. Provider responsibilities
- perform the scoped work with reasonable skill and care;
- keep client information confidential;
- flag obvious risk boundaries;
- avoid making regulated professional determinations.

7. Fees and payment
- fixed fee or hourly rate;
- VAT treatment to be verified;
- payment schedule;
- late payment terms;
- expenses approval.

8. Change control
- out-of-scope work requires written change order;
- timeline and price may change.

9. IP and license
- pre-existing IP remains with original owner;
- deliverable ownership/license terms;
- open-source dependencies remain under their licenses.

10. Confidentiality
- definition of confidential information;
- exclusions;
- permitted disclosures.

11. Data processing
- whether personal data is processed;
- DPA needed or not;
- client remains responsible for data accuracy/legal basis unless agreed otherwise;
- no sensitive data unless explicitly agreed and secured.

12. Warranty disclaimer
- no guarantee of business results, platform approval, regulatory compliance, tax outcome, revenue, growth, or error-free operation unless explicitly agreed.

13. Limitation of liability
- liability cap;
- excluded damages;
- carve-outs required by law;
- no personal guarantee.

14. Termination
- notice;
- payment for work performed;
- handover.

15. Publicity / case study
- no public use of client name/logo/quote without written permission.

16. Governing law and disputes
- to be reviewed by lawyer.
```

## 5. Clauses requiring lawyer review

- limitation of liability;
- consequential damages exclusion;
- indemnity boundaries;
- IP ownership/license;
- data processing/DPA;
- confidentiality;
- warranty disclaimer;
- governing law/forum;
- consumer vs business status;
- subcontractor liability;
- insurance requirements;
- termination/payment on cancellation.

## 6. Proof / case study permission

Before using any client proof:

- exact quote approved;
- logo/name approved;
- channels approved;
- duration approved;
- contact/withdrawal route recorded;
- sensitive claims reviewed;
- storage location documented.

Use the testimonial permission register in `appendix/register-templates.md`.

## 7. Insurance readiness

Before material consulting revenue:

- [ ] discuss professional liability insurance;
- [ ] discuss business liability insurance;
- [ ] discuss cyber coverage if handling client data;
- [ ] confirm US/Canada exclusions;
- [ ] confirm subcontractor coverage;
- [ ] confirm software/advice exclusions;
- [ ] confirm regulated-domain exclusions.

## 8. Consulting acceptance decision tree

```text
Is the project low-risk and scoped? no → reject/escalate
Does it involve regulated advice? yes → reject/escalate
Does it process sensitive data? yes → legal/privacy/security review
Does it require uncapped liability or personal guarantee? yes → reject
Can it create proof/product learning? no → consider declining
Is there a written SOW? no → do not start
Is payment/invoicing/VAT path clear? no → resolve first
```

## 9. First consulting package draft

### Product Trust-Shell Sprint

**Audience:** solo founders / small teams with a product or service about to go public.

**Deliverables:**

- official site structure;
- account architecture;
- support/privacy/contact routing;
- claims register starter;
- launch-readiness checklist;
- proof asset inventory;
- risk notes.

**Explicit exclusions:**

- legal advice;
- tax advice;
- privacy-law opinion;
- compliance certification;
- platform approval guarantee;
- ongoing support operations.

**Why it fits:** creates cashflow, uses current docs, supports brand thesis, and stays relatively low-risk if scoped correctly.
