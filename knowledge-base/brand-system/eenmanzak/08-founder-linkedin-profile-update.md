# Founder LinkedIn Profile Update Plan

**Status:** Draft
**Last updated:** 2026-04-17
**Reference profile:** https://www.linkedin.com/in/ashwinspurohit/
**Source IDs:** U07, L03-L09, E01-E04, E25

> Goal: update the founder profile in subtle but high-impact ways so it still reads as a serious working senior engineer, while clearly showing founder/operator identity and routing trust to the official company site.

## Positioning principle

Do not turn the profile into hype. Make it read like:

> “I am a senior software engineer with a serious day-job track record, and I am also building a founder-led product company with clear public proof, official links, and a focused portfolio.”

That balance matters because the founder context includes full-time employment, Dutch eenmanszaak planning, product work, and sensitive legal/tax/immigration boundaries. The LinkedIn profile should be credible, precise, and non-confusing.

## Profile goals

| Goal | Why it matters | Profile surface |
|---|---|---|
| Keep employment credibility | Trust and seniority | current role, experience, skills |
| Add founder identity | Brand trust transfer | headline, about, featured, experience/project section |
| Link official company site | Avoid fake/duplicate accounts and route traffic | contact info, featured, company Page, about CTA |
| Show product proof | Proves shipping, not just intent | featured demos, product pages, GitHub/YouTube |
| Avoid overclaiming | Compliance and employer/trust safety | wording, disclaimers, exact status |

## Recommended headline variants

Pick one depending on how public the company should be today.

### Option A — safest / subtle

```text
Senior Software Engineer | Building practical software for solo operators, accessibility, and AI-assisted workflows
```

### Option B — clearer founder signal

```text
Senior Software Engineer | Founder building practical tools for one-person businesses and accessible mobile workflows
```

### Option C — strongest company linkage

```text
Senior Software Engineer | Founder, [Company Name] — practical software for solo operators and accessibility-first products
```

### Option D — Dutch/ZZP-oriented

```text
Senior Software Engineer | Founder building tools for Dutch ZZP’ers and one-person businesses
```

Recommendation: start with **Option B** until the official company site and Page are live, then move to **Option C**.

## Featured section

Add 3-5 featured items, in this order:

1. **Official company site** — the canonical trust router.
2. **Company LinkedIn Page** — once live.
3. **Best product demo** — YouTube or landing page.
4. **GitHub organization** — if public and clean.
5. **Flagship founder essay/post** — “why I’m building this.”

Avoid featuring half-finished legal/tax docs, private WBSO strategy, or stale product pages.

## Contact and links

Update:

- Website: official company site.
- Company Page: official LinkedIn Page once created.
- Email: business email only if ready for inbound.
- GitHub: founder or company org, depending on public cleanliness.
- YouTube: company Brand Channel once demo content exists.

## Activity strategy after profile update

For the first 30 days after updating profile:

- Pin/feature one founder intro post.
- Publish 2-3 posts/week.
- Comment on 10 relevant posts/week.
- Avoid heavy product selling until the official site and support/privacy surfaces are ready.
- Use profile views and inbound messages as early signal.

## Red lines

Do not claim:

- official government/KVK endorsement,
- guaranteed tax/WBSO outcomes,
- legal/accounting advice,
- product launch status before it is true,
- revenue/user metrics without evidence,
- “team” scale that does not exist.

Do not link:

- private WBSO/tax docs,
- stale product pages,
- unreviewed privacy/terms,
- abandoned GitHub repos,
- unmaintained social accounts.
