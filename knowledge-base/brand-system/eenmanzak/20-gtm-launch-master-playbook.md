# GTM and Launch Master Playbook

**Status:** Draft; deployable GTM operating guide
**Last updated:** 2026-04-17
**Purpose:** Define how the company should go from research to offer to launch to repeatable distribution for apps, micro-SaaS, and consulting.

## 1. GTM principle

Do not launch a product. Launch a **proof system**:

```text
Positioning → proof page → demo → support route → launch story → reply loop → learning synthesis → next iteration
```

Every launch should produce:

- customers or conversations;
- objections;
- reusable proof;
- a clearer ICP;
- a decision to continue, narrow, pause, or kill.

## 2. GTM stages

| Stage | Question | Output | Gate |
|---|---|---|---|
| Research | Who has a painful problem now? | customer-language board | 10+ concrete problem signals |
| Positioning | What are they comparing us to? | positioning statement | user can repeat it back |
| Offer | What can they say yes to? | CTA / waitlist / paid offer | clear next action |
| Proof | Why trust us? | demo, source, case, screenshot, decision record | proof supports claim |
| Launch | Where will they discover it? | launch pack | support/reply plan ready |
| Learn | What did reality say? | launch report | continue/narrow/pause decision |

## 3. GTM motion by business lane

### A. Consulting / service GTM

Best first GTM if revenue/proof is urgent.

Offer shape:

- Product Trust-Shell Sprint;
- AI Workflow Audit;
- App Store Readiness Review;
- Automation Prototype Sprint.

Channels:

- founder LinkedIn;
- warm network;
- direct outreach to relevant founders/operators;
- company proof page;
- private proposals.

Launch assets:

- service page;
- SOW template;
- 3 proof examples;
- founder post;
- 20 warm outreach messages;
- case-study permission template.

Success signal:

- 3–5 qualified calls;
- 1 paid pilot;
- repeated pain language;
- one permissioned proof asset.

### B. Web micro-SaaS GTM

Best once repeated workflow pain is visible.

Offer shape:

- checklist/template first;
- waitlist second;
- paid beta third;
- self-serve SaaS later.

Channels:

- founder LinkedIn;
- SEO/source-linked pages;
- Reddit/listening communities;
- Product Hunt when product is demo-ready;
- newsletter after opt-in and cadence exist.

Launch assets:

- landing page;
- demo or prototype;
- source-linked FAQ;
- payment path decision;
- support/privacy/terms;
- Product Hunt draft;
- 20–50 warm messages.

Success signal:

- 20+ waitlist signups from target audience;
- 5+ interviews;
- paid commitment or pilot;
- repeated willingness-to-pay language.

### C. Mobile app GTM

Best for TapTap-style product proof and paid consumer/app-store distribution.

Offer shape:

- App Store listing;
- demo video;
- founder story;
- caregiver/senior benefit framing;
- subscription only after value is clear.

Channels:

- App Store search;
- YouTube demos;
- Facebook/Pinterest later for TapTap audience;
- founder/company LinkedIn as proof;
- Product Hunt only if tech/startup audience is relevant.

Launch assets:

- app-store screenshots;
- app preview video;
- privacy/support/terms;
- RevenueCat/test purchase proof;
- FAQ;
- support inbox;
- launch post and demo.

Success signal:

- App Store approval;
- clean onboarding;
- activation/retention signal;
- support load manageable;
- conversion or learning from pricing test.

## 4. Channel selection rules

Use a channel only if:

- target audience is present;
- the company can post/reply consistently;
- channel outputs can be measured;
- trust risk is acceptable;
- claims can be controlled.

Early channel priority:

1. founder LinkedIn;
2. company website;
3. company LinkedIn;
4. GitHub/YouTube proof;
5. Reddit listening;
6. direct/warm outreach;
7. Product Hunt for launch-ready products;
8. SEO/resource pages after positioning stabilizes;
9. paid/creator channels only after proof exists.

## 5. Launch asset checklist

For any launch:

- [ ] one-line positioning;
- [ ] target ICP;
- [ ] problem statement in customer language;
- [ ] competitive alternative;
- [ ] proof/demo;
- [ ] pricing or CTA;
- [ ] support route;
- [ ] privacy/terms/refund/cancel route if paid;
- [ ] claims register reviewed;
- [ ] founder launch post;
- [ ] company launch post;
- [ ] email/waitlist message if applicable;
- [ ] Product Hunt/HN/Reddit assets if applicable;
- [ ] reply templates;
- [ ] launch-day response schedule;
- [ ] post-launch report template.

## 6. Launch day response system

### First 4 hours

- reply to all comments/questions;
- capture objections;
- fix broken links/support paths;
- update FAQ if the same question repeats;
- route sensitive issues to support/privacy email.

### First 48 hours

- publish “what we learned” update;
- contact warm leads;
- tag all objections;
- check analytics/conversions;
- identify top 3 friction points.

### First 14 days

- decide continue/narrow/pause;
- improve landing copy;
- collect permissioned proof;
- write launch retrospective;
- update product roadmap or kill criteria.

## 7. Metrics by stage

| Stage | Metrics |
|---|---|
| Research | number of real conversations, repeated pains, exact phrases, objections |
| Waitlist | visits, signup rate, qualified signups, reply rate |
| Service | qualified calls, proposal rate, close rate, delivery risk |
| App | install, activation, trial start, conversion, retention, support tickets |
| SaaS | signup, activation, paid conversion, churn, support load |
| Content | meaningful comments, profile visits, site clicks, saves, replies |

## 9. GTM anti-patterns

- launching without support/privacy/terms;
- broad audience before narrow proof;
- posting on many channels with no reply plan;
- Product Hunt launch without demo/proof;
- paid ads before organic message clarity;
- fake urgency/scarcity;
- using AI to spam comments/DMs;
- treating waitlist signups as payment validation;
- ignoring objections because engagement looked good.
