# GovProcure Competitive Intelligence

## GovWin IQ (Deltek) — Primary Incumbent
- **Price**: $15K-$50K/year (enterprise); ~$40K typical
- **Owner**: Deltek (private equity-backed enterprise software)
- **Market position**: 20+ year incumbent, market leader
- **Features**: Opportunity database, bid alerts, company intel, analyst reports, team collab
- **Why they charge $40K**: Data quality (historical + real-time), analyst team, compliance
- **Pain points** (churn intel):
  - Slow data updates (batch, not real-time)
  - Requires weeks of training
  - UI is outdated, not developer-friendly
  - No API access at lower tiers
  - Expensive — unaffordable for small firms (<50 employees)
- **Achilles heel**: Legacy architecture prevents real-time data; price locks out 80% of market

## Tango — Emerging Threat
- **Price**: Unknown (estimated $5K-$20K/year based on positioning)
- **Positioning**: API-first, emerging competitor
- **Status**: Less comprehensive database, faster implementation
- **Risk to GovProcure**: Competing in same "cheaper than GovWin" space

## USAspending.gov — Free Baseline
- **Price**: Free (government-maintained)
- **Why contractors still pay**: Raw data has errors, duplicates, no alerts, poor UX
- **Our moat vs. free**: Deduplication, API standardization, alerts, enrichment, UX

## Our Asymmetric Advantages
1. **Price**: 10-100x cheaper than GovWin — opens market to 80% of GovCon firms
2. **API-native**: Developers don't buy seats — they query an API → B2D motion
3. **Agent-native**: Async work, automation, webhook support
4. **Real-time**: Built on APIs → faster than GovWin's batch processing
5. **Transparent pricing**: Monthly, self-serve, no sales call required

## Displacement Targets (Who Complains About GovWin)
- Small 8(a) firms that can't afford $40K/year
- Solo capture managers at 10-50 person firms
- BD consultants who work across multiple clients
- New entrants to government contracting (no incumbent relationship)
