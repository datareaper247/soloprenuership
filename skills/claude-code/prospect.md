# Prospect — Research + Outreach + Sequence in One Shot

## Auto-Trigger (No Slash Command Needed)
Fires automatically when founder says: "cold outreach to [company/role]", "prospect [X]", "find leads at [company]", "write outreach for [target]"

---


**Usage**: `/prospect "[company name] [role/title] [optional context]"`

**Examples**:
- `/prospect "Acme Corp VP Engineering recently raised Series A"`
- `/prospect "Shopify Plus merchant selling outdoor gear, founder"`
- `/prospect "Notion VP Growth high-growth SaaS"`
- `/prospect "Local law firm 10-person looking for document automation"`

The single most hated task in solo founder life: cold outreach. This skill eliminates the friction between "I should reach out to this person" and having a personalized, researched, ready-to-send message.

---

## What Makes Prospect Different From `/sales outreach`

`/sales outreach` takes a context you provide and writes email variants.

`/prospect` **builds the context for you first** — it researches the target, infers their pain, builds the personalization hook, then generates the outreach — all from a company name and role.

**The chain**: `Research → Pain Hypothesis → Personalization Hook → 3 Email Variants → 6-Touch Sequence → CRM Note Template`

This is the difference between generic outreach and outreach that gets replies.

## The Prospect Chain

### Phase 1: Target Research (2 minutes)

From the company + role provided, infer or research:

**Company Intel**:
- Company stage (startup/growth/enterprise — infer from known info)
- Primary business model (SaaS/services/ecommerce/marketplace)
- Known recent events (funding, product launches, new hires, press)
- Primary revenue driver and customer base
- Tech stack signals (if visible from job postings, website)

**Role Intel**:
- Standard responsibilities for this title at this company stage
- What metrics they're accountable for
- What keeps this role up at night (universal pain by title)
- What "good" looks like for them (their definition of success)

**Pain Hypothesis** (the core of all good outreach):
Apply the **Pain Stack** by title:
```
VP Engineering: reliability, recruiting, technical debt, build vs buy
VP Growth / CMO: CAC, attribution, content at scale, channel saturation
CEO/Founder: everything (prioritize: revenue, team, capital efficiency)
VP Sales: quota attainment, ramp time, conversion, tool sprawl
Product Manager: prioritization, stakeholder alignment, metric ownership
Operations: process gaps, systems that don't talk, manual work at scale
CFO: burn rate, unit economics, forecast accuracy
```

### Phase 2: Personalization Hook (The Research Unlock)

The hook is the single most important element. It's the proof that you did 5 minutes of research instead of mass-blasting.

**Hook sources** (in priority order):
1. Recent press/funding announcement — "Congrats on [round] — companies in that stage typically..."
2. Recent product launch — "Saw the [product] launch — [observation about what it signals]..."
3. Hiring signal — "Noticed you're hiring a [role] — teams scaling that position often..."
4. Content they published — "Your [post/talk] about [topic] resonated because..."
5. Competitor signal — "Saw [competitor] just [did X] — means your market is..."
6. Pain signal from review sites — "Found some [G2/Capterra] reviews mentioning [pain] at [company]..."

If no research signal is available, use the **Title-Stage Default Hook**:
"[Title]s at [stage] companies almost universally run into [universal pain]. We see this most with teams that [trigger condition]..."

### Phase 3: Value Bridge

Connect the hook to what you do without pitching:

**Bridge formula**: "[Hook observation] → This usually means [pain you solve] → Companies like [similar customer] used [your solution] to [specific result]"

Keep it 1-2 sentences. Not a pitch. A connection.

### Phase 4: Outreach Generation

Generate 3 variants (same as `/sales outreach` but with the research built in):

**Variant 1 — PAS + Research Hook**
Uses the personalization hook + Pain-Agitate-Solution

**Variant 2 — Insight Lead**
Opens with a market observation or data point specific to their situation
Does NOT mention your product until the last line

**Variant 3 — Ultra-Short (LinkedIn)**
3 sentences. Hook + connection + one question. No pitch.

### Phase 5: Full Sequence (6 Touches)

After the opening message, full 6-touch sequence over 21 days.
Each touch adds NEW value — no "just following up":

- Day 1: Opening (one of the 3 variants)
- Day 4: LinkedIn connection request (2 sentences)
- Day 7: Value add (share relevant insight, resource, or case study)
- Day 10: LinkedIn message post-connection (comment on their content or company news)
- Day 14: ROI or urgency angle
- Day 21: Break-up email (permission to say no — highest reply rate)

## The Research Enhancement Mode

### `/prospect --deep "[company]"`

When you need more than inference — for high-value prospects:

Add to the research phase:
1. **Glassdoor signals**: What do employees say about challenges there?
2. **Job posting analysis**: What are they hiring for? Signals pain points.
3. **Review site mining**: G2/Capterra/Trustpilot for product companies
4. **LinkedIn signals**: Recent posts from the target, company updates
5. **Press coverage**: What story does the media tell about them?

Output includes `DEEP RESEARCH NOTES` section with specific findings.

## Smart Personalization Rules

### The Specificity Ladder
More specific = higher reply rate. Always aim for the highest rung available:

```
Level 1 (Generic — lowest reply): "I help companies like yours with [pain]"
Level 2 (Title-based): "VP Engineers at Series B companies typically struggle with..."
Level 3 (Stage + event): "Post-Series A teams rebuilding their tech stack often..."
Level 4 (Specific hook): "Saw your TechCrunch coverage of your API launch..."
Level 5 (Hyper-specific): "Your post about [topic] hit on something we see constantly..."
```

### Length Calibration
- First email: 75-100 words max
- LinkedIn DM: 3 sentences max
- Subject line: Under 7 words (mobile preview optimization)
- CTA: One ask only — never give multiple options

### The Trigger Event Multiplier
Messages sent within 48 hours of a trigger event get 3x higher open rates.
Trigger events: funding, product launch, new hire announcement, press coverage, leadership change.

**Parallel execution**:

## Anti-Patterns This Prevents

- **Spray and pray**: Generic emails to 500 contacts → 0.1% reply rate
- **Research paralysis**: Spending 2 hours researching one prospect
- **Pitch-first failure**: Leading with your product before establishing relevance
- **"Just following up" fatigue**: Follow-ups with no new value
- **No sequence**: Sending one email and waiting forever

**Target metrics with this skill**:
- Reply rate: 8-15% (industry baseline: 1-3%)
- Positive reply rate: 3-6%
- Time per prospect: 5 minutes vs 45 minutes manual
