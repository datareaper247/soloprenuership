# Content — Professional Content Production

**Usage**: `/content [type] "[topic or brief]"`

Produces professional-grade content assets with the correct format, length, tone, and optimization for each content type. Every piece is structured, purposeful, and ready for minimal editing.

---

## Content Types

### `blog` — SEO-Optimized Long-Form Blog Post
**Usage**: `/content blog "[topic or keyword]"`

#### Pre-Production Checklist
1. Identify the target keyword and search intent (informational/commercial/transactional)
2. Determine the SERP landscape — what's currently ranking and why
3. Define the unique angle (why is OUR take different or better)
4. Outline the H2/H3 structure before writing
5. Identify LSI keywords to weave naturally

#### Structure Template
```
[POST TITLE — includes primary keyword, <65 characters]
Meta Description: [150-160 chars, includes keyword, compelling CTA]
Target Keyword: [keyword | monthly volume | difficulty]
Content Goal: [SEO rank / thought leadership / link bait / lead gen]
Estimated Read Time: X minutes

---

INTRODUCTION (150-200 words)
- Hook: [surprising stat, provocative question, or bold claim]
- Context: [why this matters now]
- Promise: [exactly what reader will learn]
- NOT: "In this article, we will..." (cliché) — be bold and direct

H2: [Keyword-rich section title]
(300-500 words per H2)
- Use data, examples, specifics
- Break up with bullet points where appropriate
- Internal link opportunity: [related page]

H2: [Second major point]
...

H2: [Third major point]
...

H2: Common Questions About [Topic] (FAQ section)
- Q: [Question as people search it]
  A: [Direct, complete answer in 2-4 sentences]
[3-5 FAQ questions]

CONCLUSION (150-200 words)
- Summary of key insights
- Takeaway: [1 actionable sentence]
- CTA: [specific next step for reader]

---
INTERNAL LINKS: [2-3 relevant internal pages to link to]
EXTERNAL LINKS: [1-2 authoritative sources to cite]
FEATURED IMAGE ALT TEXT: [keyword-descriptive]
SCHEMA MARKUP: [Article / FAQ / HowTo as appropriate]
```

#### SEO Quality Gates
- Primary keyword in: title, first 100 words, one H2, meta description
- Word count: 1,500-3,000 words (depending on competitive landscape)
- Reading level: Grade 8-10 (tools like Hemingway)
- No keyword stuffing — natural integration only
- Every H2 answers a specific question

### `email-sequence` — Lifecycle Email Sequence
**Usage**: `/content email-sequence "[sequence goal] for [audience]"`

Sequence types: welcome, trial onboarding, trial-to-paid conversion, re-engagement, post-purchase, upsell, churn prevention, win-back

#### Framework: The PASTOR Method
- **P**roblem: Open with their pain
- **A**mplify: Make the problem feel real and urgent
- **S**tory/Solution: Bridge with relevant story or proof
- **T**estimony: Social proof
- **O**ffer: Clear offer/ask
- **R**esponse: Specific CTA

#### Email Structure
```
SEQUENCE NAME: [Name]
GOAL: [What we want reader to DO at end of sequence]
TRIGGER: [What action starts this sequence]
AUDIENCE SEGMENT: [Who receives this]

---

EMAIL 1: [Name] — Day 0
Subject Line Options:
  A: [Option A — benefit-focused]
  B: [Option B — curiosity-focused]
  C: [Option C — direct]
Preview Text: [Max 90 chars, complements subject]
Goal: [What this email accomplishes]

[EMAIL BODY]
---
[Signed-off by a person, not "The Team"]
CTA: [Single, specific CTA button text and destination]
P.S.: [Optional — reinforce CTA or add curiosity hook]

---

EMAIL 2: [Name] — Day [X]
Subject: ...
[body]

[Continue for all emails in sequence]

---

SEQUENCE PERFORMANCE METRICS TO TRACK
- Open rate target: X%
- Click rate target: X%
- Conversion rate target: X%
- Unsubscribe rate acceptable max: X%

A/B TEST PLAN
Email 1: Test subject line A vs B
Email 3: Test CTA button copy
```

#### Sequence Length Guidelines
- Welcome: 3-5 emails over 7 days
- Trial onboarding: 5-7 emails over 14 days
- Re-engagement: 3 emails over 7 days
- Win-back: 3 emails over 14 days

#### Tone Rules
- Write to ONE person (not "you all")
- Sentences under 20 words where possible
- One idea per email
- One CTA per email
- Never start with "I" (start with "You" or action word)

### `landing-page` — Conversion-Focused Landing Page
**Usage**: `/content landing-page "[product or offer]"`

#### Above-the-Fold Framework (First 5 seconds)
Every element answers: "What is it? Who is it for? Why should I care?"

```
LANDING PAGE: [Product Name]
Campaign: [Source / Offer]
Goal: [Signup / Trial / Demo / Purchase]
Primary CTA: [Button text]
Target conversion rate: X%

---

HERO SECTION
Headline: [Value proposition — specific outcome, not feature]
  Max 8 words. Must contain the result, not the method.
  Good: "Close 2x More Deals Without Changing Your Process"
  Bad: "AI-Powered Sales Automation Platform"

Subheadline: [1-2 sentences expanding on headline — who it's for + how]

Social proof line: [X customers / Y in savings / Z% improvement]

CTA Button: [Action verb + outcome or next step]
  Good: "Start My Free Trial" / "Get My Report" / "Book a Demo"
  Bad: "Submit" / "Click Here" / "Learn More"

Hero image/video alt text: [What visual shows]

---

PROBLEM SECTION (optional but powerful)
"If you're [persona], you know the pain of [specific problem]..."
[3 bullets describing the pain state]

---

SOLUTION SECTION
"[Product] makes [desired outcome] [easier/faster/cheaper/possible]"
[3-4 key benefits — outcomes not features]
| Benefit | Description | Proof point |

---

SOCIAL PROOF SECTION
Logo bar: [Customer logos if B2B]
Testimonials (3 minimum):
  Structure: [Specific result + context + name/title/company]
  "We reduced onboarding time from 3 weeks to 2 days using [Product]."
  — Jane Smith, Head of Operations, Acme Corp

Stats block: [3 compelling numbers]

---

FEATURES SECTION (below fold — for those who need details)
Feature → Benefit format:
[Feature name]: [What you can do] → [Result you get]

---

FAQ SECTION
[5-7 questions that overcome the most common objections]
Q: How long does it take to set up?
A: [Specific, reassuring answer]

---

FINAL CTA SECTION
Headline: [Re-state the value]
CTA: [Same or stronger CTA]
Risk reversal: [Money-back guarantee / No credit card / Cancel anytime]

---

PAGE METADATA
Title: [Keyword-rich, <65 chars]
Meta description: [CTA-driven, <160 chars]
OG Title: [Social share title]
OG Description: [Social share description]
```

## Content Quality Standards

### Non-Negotiables for Every Piece
1. **Specific over generic**: "Reduced churn by 23%" beats "reduced churn significantly"
2. **Active voice**: "We built X" not "X was built by us"
3. **One idea per paragraph** (especially email and social)
4. **Read aloud test**: If it sounds unnatural spoken, rewrite it
5. **Benefit > Feature**: Lead with what the reader GETS, not what the product DOES
6. **No jargon without explanation**: Define technical terms for the target audience
7. **CTA clarity**: Exactly one desired action per piece; make it obvious

### Tone Calibration
- B2B SaaS: Professional but conversational; avoid corporate-speak
- Consumer: Warm, relatable, slightly informal
- Developer audience: Direct, technical, no fluff, respect their intelligence
- Executive audience: Data-driven, concise, outcome-focused

### The "So What" Test
For every paragraph: ask "So what does this mean for the reader?" If you can't answer, cut or rewrite.
