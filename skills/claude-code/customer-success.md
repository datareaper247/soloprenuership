# CUSTOMER-SUCCESS — Early-Stage CS Engine

## Auto-Trigger (No Slash Command Needed)

Fires automatically when founder says:
- "customer is unhappy" / "customer is churning" / "at-risk account"
- "how do I onboard my customers"
- "customers aren't using the product" / "low engagement after signup"
- "what do I do after a customer signs up"
- "customer support is overwhelming me"
- "how do I retain customers"
- "customer health" / "account health"
- "expansion opportunity" / "how do I upsell"
- "customer doesn't see value" / "they're not getting ROI"
- "customer asked for a refund"

**Customer Success is not customer support.** Support is reactive — fix what's broken. CS is proactive — ensure customers get the value they paid for before they realize they're not getting it.

**CS fires at a critical moment**: The first 90 days after a customer signs up determine whether they stay for years or churn. No amount of acquisition skill compensates for failing at this window.

---

## WHY THIS EXISTS

Lincoln Murphy (Customer Success author, Gainsight research) found:
- 90% of customer lifetime value comes after the initial sale
- Customers who reach their "first value moment" in <7 days have 3x the lifetime value of those who don't
- The churn decision is typically made in the first 30-45 days — before the cancellation notice arrives

**The solo founder CS paradox**: You're the product, the sales person, AND the CS team. Every minute on CS is a minute not building. This skill systematizes CS so it takes 20% of the time and delivers 90% of the retention impact.

## THE FIRST 90 DAYS FRAMEWORK

The most important period in any customer relationship.

```
90-DAY CS CALENDAR:
━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━

DAY 0 (Sign-up day):
□ Founder sends personal welcome email (not automated)
□ Customer added to onboarding sequence
□ Onboarding call scheduled (for any ACV >$100/mo)

DAY 1-7 (CRITICAL — Time to First Value):
□ Confirm customer attempted to use core feature
□ If no activity by day 3: trigger activation email
□ If activity: confirm they reached the Aha Moment
□ Onboarding call happens (if ACV warrants)
□ Goal: Customer has one concrete success story by day 7

DAY 7-30 (Habit Formation):
□ Weekly check-in (async — email or Loom video, not call)
□ Share 1 tip/use case relevant to their profile each week
□ Monitor: are they returning? If not: proactive outreach
□ Goal: Product is in their weekly workflow

DAY 30-60 (Value Demonstration):
□ First value review: "What's working? What's not?"
□ Document a specific win they've had (becomes your case study)
□ Identify: Are there team members they should introduce to the product?
□ Goal: Customer can articulate ROI in their own words

DAY 60-90 (Expansion and Loyalty):
□ NPS survey (if haven't done yet)
□ Expansion conversation: "What would make this even more valuable for you?"
□ Referral ask (only if NPS ≥8): "Is there someone else who'd benefit from this?"
□ Goal: Customer is an advocate, not just a user
```

## THE AHA MOMENT PROTOCOL

The Aha Moment is the specific instant a customer first receives real value. **Everything in early CS is about accelerating time-to-Aha.**

```
AHA MOMENT IDENTIFICATION:
━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━

Step 1: Define YOUR product's Aha Moment precisely.
Format: "The customer [specific action] and [specific positive outcome happens]."

Examples by product type:
- Invoice software: "Customer sends their first invoice and it arrives in the client's inbox"
- Analytics: "Customer sees their first insight that they act on"
- Project tool: "Customer completes their first task and the status updates automatically"
- Email tool: "Customer receives their first reply generated by the AI draft"

Step 2: Measure time-to-Aha for each cohort.
- Set up event tracking for the specific action that defines the Aha Moment
- Calculate: time from signup to that event for every activated user
- Target by product type:
  Consumer: <24 hours
  SMB SaaS: <3 days
  Mid-market: <1 week
  Enterprise: <2 weeks

Step 3: For customers who haven't reached Aha by day 3 — intervene.
See the Activation Rescue Protocol below.

Step 4: Use Aha Moment data to optimize onboarding.
"Which step in the onboarding flow most prevents reaching the Aha Moment?"
Kill or simplify that step. Measure if time-to-Aha improves.
```

## THE PERSONAL WELCOME EMAIL (Template)

The highest-leverage CS action for any founder. Send within 2 hours of signup. Not automated.

```
PERSONAL WELCOME EMAIL:
━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━

Subject: Welcome to [Product] — quick question from the founder

Body:

Hi [First name],

I'm [Your name], the founder of [Product]. I saw you just signed up — welcome.

Quick question before you dive in: what were you hoping [Product] would help you with?

I ask because we set up customers differently depending on what they're trying to accomplish,
and I want to make sure you're starting in the right place.

(This isn't a sales email — I'm not pitching anything. I just read every reply personally.)

[Your name]
P.S. If you hit any friction in the first few days, reply directly. I'm the one answering.

---

PERSONALIZATION RULE: If you know something about them (company name, how they found you,
what they signed up for), add one sentence referencing it.
"I noticed you came from [source] — [relevant thing about that]."

RESPONSE RATE TARGET: 15-35% for this email.
If <10%: subject line or sender name is the problem.
If >35%: you're targeting an ICP with a very acute need (great sign).
```

## ONBOARDING CALL PROTOCOL

**When to have them**: Any customer paying >$100/month deserves a live onboarding.
**Goal**: Confirm they can reach the Aha Moment with your help.
**Duration**: 20-30 minutes max.

```
ONBOARDING CALL AGENDA:
━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━

MIN 1-5: DISCOVERY (them talking, you listening)
"Before I show you anything: tell me what you're hoping to accomplish with [Product]."
"What does your current process look like for [problem you solve]?"
"What would success look like 30 days from now?"

Why this order: Most founders start by showing the product. Wrong.
               Learn their specific situation first — then show ONLY the parts that matter to THEM.

MIN 5-20: GUIDED FIRST SUCCESS
Show them the ONE path from where they are to their first Aha Moment.
Not a full product tour. Not feature showcase. ONE path.
"Based on what you told me, here's exactly what I'd do first."
[Guide them through it live, on their account, with their real data if possible]

MIN 20-25: FRICTION ACKNOWLEDGMENT
"What was confusing or unclear about what we just did?"
"Is there anything that would have stopped you from getting here on your own?"
Write down the answers — these are your onboarding improvements.

MIN 25-30: NEXT STEPS
"In the next 7 days, here's the ONE thing I'd focus on: [specific action]."
"I'll check in with you in [5-7 days]. If you get stuck before then, reply to my email."
Don't give a list of things. One thing.

POST-CALL:
Send recap within 30 minutes:
"Great talking with you. Here's what we covered: [2 sentences]. Your one next step is [X]."
Include your direct email: "Reply here if anything comes up."
```

## ACTIVATION RESCUE PROTOCOL

Fires when a customer has not reached the Aha Moment within 3-5 days.

```
ACTIVATION RESCUE SEQUENCE:
━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━

DAY 3 (no core action taken):
Email 1: Obstacle removal
Subject: "Getting started with [Product] — any friction?"

"Hi [Name], I noticed you haven't [done the first thing in our product] yet.
 Completely normal — people often get stuck at [most common sticking point].

 Here's the fastest way to [Aha Moment]: [1-2 sentence guide]

 If something's getting in the way, just reply — takes me 5 minutes to sort it out.

 [Name]"

DAY 7 (still no core action):
Email 2: Value reminder + specific help
Subject: "Quick tip for [their specific use case]"

"Hi [Name], quick thing that's helped customers like [similar role/company]:
 [Specific tip tailored to their situation from signup data]

 Takes about 5 minutes to set up. Let me know if you want me to walk you through it.

 [Name]"

DAY 14 (still no core action):
Email 3: Direct intervention
Subject: "Should we reschedule?"

"Hi [Name], I want to make sure [Product] is actually useful for you.

 A few customers who haven't activated yet tell me they either:
 (a) Got busy and haven't had a chance to try it yet
 (b) Tried it and got confused about [common confusing thing]
 (c) Realized it might not be the right fit

 Which one is it for you? (Even 'c' is a useful answer — saves us both time.)

 [Name]"

DAY 21 (still no core action):
Final email: Exit interview offer
Subject: "One last question"

"Hi [Name], I've tried to reach you a few times — I'll stop after this one.

 I'm genuinely curious: what made you sign up, and what happened?
 Your answer will help the next person who's in your situation.

 One sentence in reply is all I need. No pressure either way.

 [Name]"
```

## CUSTOMER HEALTH SCORING (Minimum Viable Version)

Even with 10 customers, you need to know who's at risk.

```
SIMPLE HEALTH SCORE (solo founder version):
━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━

SIGNALS (check weekly, takes 10 minutes):

🟢 HEALTHY (all 3 true):
□ Used the core feature this week
□ Last support ticket resolved / no open complaints
□ Renewed or has not expressed price concerns

🟡 WATCH (any 1 true):
□ No usage in 7-10 days (non-holiday week)
□ Open complaint / unresolved issue >48h
□ Asked about pricing / referenced cancellation language
□ Hasn't responded to 2+ check-in emails

🔴 AT-RISK (any 1 true):
□ No usage in 14+ days
□ Expressed direct dissatisfaction
□ Asked how to cancel or export their data
□ Downgraded or requested downgrade

FOR EACH 🔴 ACCOUNT: Founder personal outreach within 24 hours.
"I saw [specific signal] — I wanted to reach out directly. What's going on?"

TOOL REQUIREMENT: You don't need a CS platform. You need a spreadsheet:
[Customer name] | [Last login date] | [Status] | [Last action taken] | [Notes]
Review this weekly. 10 minutes. The most valuable 10 minutes in your business.
```

## THE ESCALATION PROTOCOL (When Things Go Wrong)

```
COMPLAINT ESCALATION LADDER:
━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━

LEVEL 1: Product complaint (feature doesn't work as expected)
Response time: <4 hours
Owner: Whoever sees it first
Resolution: Fix + follow-up confirmation they're unblocked

LEVEL 2: Service failure (we caused them to lose time / money / customers)
Response time: <1 hour
Owner: Founder personally
Script: "I want to take responsibility for this directly. Here's what happened, why it
        happened, and what I'm doing to make sure it doesn't happen again: [specifics].
        And here's what I'm doing to make this right for you: [compensation/credit/fix]."
Resolution: Compensation + post-resolution check-in at day 7

LEVEL 3: Churn intention stated
Response time: <30 minutes if possible
Owner: Founder personally, phone call preferred
Script: "I got your message. Can I call you in the next hour? I want to understand
        what's happened and see if there's something I can do."
Goal: NOT to save the revenue (yet). To understand the real reason.
      "What would we have needed to do differently for this to have worked?"
      The answer to this is your most valuable product feedback.

LEVEL 4: Refund request
Response time: Same day
Policy: For any customer <90 days: honor the refund, ask one question.
"I've processed your refund. Before you go — what one thing would you tell the next
 person who's considering [Product]?"
Why: Refunds are expensive. The feedback is worth the refund cost twice over.
     AND: customers who get clean refunds often come back, or refer people.
     Customers who get fought over refunds never do.
```

## EXPANSION REVENUE PROTOCOL

The most efficient revenue a solo founder can generate — it comes from people who already trust you.

```
EXPANSION TRIGGERS (watch for these signals):
━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━

NATURAL EXPANSION SIGNALS:
□ Customer mentions a use case your product doesn't cover yet (adjacent need)
□ Customer invites colleagues / team members to the product (multi-seat opportunity)
□ Customer's usage has grown significantly in the last 30 days (value = growing)
□ Customer shares an outcome story with you (proof of ROI → upgrade conversation)
□ Customer asks "can [Product] do X?" where X is a premium feature (upgrade signal)

EXPANSION CONVERSATION STARTERS:
"I noticed you've been using [feature] a lot — there's a [higher tier/add-on] that extends this.
 Would it be useful if I walked you through what that adds?"

"You mentioned [business goal] last time we talked. Have you made progress on that?
 If [new feature] would help with that, we can unlock it."

"We have several customers in [similar situation] who've found that [upgrade] lets them [outcome].
 Does that sound like where you're headed?"

EXPANSION TIMING RULE:
Never expand before the customer has had their first success with the base product.
Expansion before value = upselling. Expansion after value = helping them get more of what works.
The customer should initiate the expansion or clearly signal the readiness. You facilitate.

EXPANSION FLOOR:
If a customer's NPS is < 7: fix their experience before any expansion conversation.
Expanding an unhappy customer = accelerating churn, not increasing revenue.
```

## THE REFERRAL MOMENT

```
REFERRAL PROTOCOL (only after health confirmed):
━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━

TIMING: Day 45-60, ONLY for customers who have:
□ Reached the Aha Moment
□ Used the product consistently for 3+ weeks
□ Given a positive signal (NPS ≥8, positive email reply, unprompted recommendation)

THE ASK (direct and specific):
"You've been using [Product] for [X weeks] and I love that [specific thing they said/did].
 Is there one person in your network — maybe someone in a similar role at another company —
 who has the same problem you had when you came to us?
 I'm not asking for a mass intro. Just one specific person, if anyone comes to mind."

WHY THIS WORKS: Specific > Generic.
"Do you know anyone?" → requires mental search of everyone they know
"One person in a similar role" → requires mental search of a small specific group
Smaller search = more likely to produce an answer

REFERRAL INCENTIVE (optional):
Before offering incentives: 60% of B2B referrals happen without any incentive.
Offering money for referrals sometimes lowers quality (referring people to get the reward).
Better than cash: "I'll personally onboard anyone you send — they'll have direct access to me."
```

## SOLO FOUNDER CS CALENDAR (Minimum Viable)

For founders with <50 customers, this is the entire CS system:

```
WEEKLY CS ROUTINE (Friday, 30 minutes):
━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━

□ Review health spreadsheet: flag any 🔴 accounts
□ Send personal check-in to any 🟡 accounts that have been yellow 2+ weeks
□ Log any expansion opportunities that surfaced this week
□ Review 1 churn/complaint from the week: what's the pattern?
□ Send referral ask to any customer who crossed 45 days with positive signal

MONTHLY CS REVIEW (30 minutes):
□ NRR calculation: are you at 100%+?
□ D30 retention rate: trend up or down?
□ Churn reasons (last month): what's the #1 reason?
□ Expansion MRR: which accounts expanded? Why?
□ Onboarding friction: what's the #1 step where customers get stuck?

QUARTERLY (1-2 hours):
□ Customer success stories: interview 2 healthy customers for case study material
□ ICP refinement: are your healthiest customers a specific type?
□ Pricing review: are your price points aligned with the value customers are getting?
□ CS playbook update: add/remove protocols based on what's working
```

## INTEGRATION

| Skill | Relationship |
|---|---|
| `pmf.md` | CS data is your richest PMF signal — health scores and Aha Moment data feed directly into PMF measurement |
| `growth.md` | CS gates growth — retention must be healthy before acquisition scales |
| `product-moat.md` | Moat features are identified through CS insight: what do healthy customers depend on most? |
| `sales.md` | CS insights = sales intelligence: what made customers successful → what to sell to the next one |
| `brand.md` | Customer success stories are your highest-converting content |
| `finance.md` | NRR is a CS metric with direct financial impact — track it monthly |
| `validate.md` | Customer interviews during CS reveal the next validation opportunities |

**The solo founder CS principle**: You cannot hire your way out of bad CS. The founder doing CS is the unfair advantage — customers who talk to the founder stay longer and pay more. Systematize to scale, but never remove the founder from the high-stakes moments: onboarding calls for high-ACV customers, churn saves, and referral asks.
