## Why Start at $5K MRR (Not $50K)?

**Acquirer math**: A $5K MRR business (~$60K ARR) sells for $120K–$240K (2–4x ARR) on Acquire.com.
The multiple range between a "clean" business and a "messy" business at the same ARR is 1.5–2x.
That's $60K–$120K of exit value determined entirely by habits formed in the first 12 months.

**Metric hygiene compounds**: A founder who tracks MRR/churn from month 1 has 24 months of clean data by $60K ARR. A founder who starts tracking at $50K ARR has 2 months of data. Buyers pay for history.

**Founder dependency is hardest to fix late**: The longer you are the support team, the sales team, and the product team, the more the product IS you. Reversing this at $50K MRR requires rebuilding systems under revenue pressure. At $5K, you can build the systems from scratch with low stakes.

## The 5-Dimension Exit Readiness Score

Rate each dimension at your current stage. Be honest — acquirers will find out anyway.

### Dimension 1: Metric Hygiene (0–20 points)

| Metric | Status | Points |
|---|---|---|
| MRR tracked and updated monthly | Yes/No | +5 |
| Monthly churn rate documented | Yes/No | +5 |
| LTV and CAC calculated (even rough) | Yes/No | +5 |
| Revenue attributed by acquisition channel | Yes/No | +5 |

**Score < 15**: "Acquirers pay for clean metrics. Without these, you either can't sell or sell at a discount. Every month without this data is a permanent hole in your record."

**Fix now (Stage 1)**: Set up a simple spreadsheet: date, new MRR, churned MRR, net MRR, cumulative customers. Update monthly. 10 minutes/month. Non-negotiable.

---

### Dimension 2: Founder Dependency Index (0–20 points)

| Factor | Status | Points |
|---|---|---|
| Customers do NOT contact you personally for support | Yes/No | +5 |
| Support handled via documented process (not you ad-hoc) | Yes/No | +5 |
| Product operates without your daily involvement | Yes/No | +5 |
| At least one recurring task has a written SOP | Yes/No | +5 |

**Score < 10**: "You're building a job, not a business. A business where the founder IS the product is unsellable to most buyers. High founder dependency typically reduces exit multiple by 30–50%."

**The Founder Dependency Test**: Can this business run for 2 weeks without you touching anything?
- No → dependency is critical; fix before any exit conversation
- Yes → document HOW it runs; that documentation IS the asset you're selling

**Fix now**: Identify the ONE thing only you can do. Write a 1-page SOP for it. Hand it to a test user (a new customer, a VA, anyone). This is the lowest-cost, highest-leverage exit prep action.

---

### Dimension 3: Platform Concentration Risk (0–20 points)

| Risk Factor | Status | Points |
|---|---|---|
| No single platform represents >50% of customers | Yes/No | +5 |
| No single platform represents >30% of revenue | Yes/No | +5 |
| Own an email list (not just social/platform following) | Yes/No | +5 |
| Multiple acquisition channels active | Yes/No | +5 |

**Score < 10**: ⚠️ PLATFORM CONCENTRATION CRITICAL

```
⚠️ PLATFORM CONCENTRATION RISK:
[X]% of revenue/customers depends on [platform].
Acquirer risk: one deplatforming event = [X]% revenue loss.
This is not a buyer's problem — they will either discount heavily or walk.
Platform concentration at this level requires an 18-month diversification plan.
```

**The diversification imperative**: One deplatforming event (app store removal, TikTok ban, Shopify policy change) eliminates the business overnight. This is not a theoretical risk — it has happened to hundreds of businesses per year. An acquirer who sees 60% revenue from one platform either discounts 40% or passes.

**Fix now**: Name the 3 customer acquisition channels that do NOT depend on your primary platform. Start building each. Even 10% of customers from a diversified channel changes the acquirer conversation.

---

### Dimension 4: Recurring Revenue Quality (0–20 points)

| Revenue Type | Your Revenue Mix | Points |
|---|---|---|
| Monthly recurring (MRR subscriptions) | [%] | +10 if >50% |
| Annual contracts (ARR, prepaid) | [%] | +15 if >50% |
| One-time or project revenue | [%] | +0 |
| Net Revenue Retention >100% | Yes/No | +5 bonus |

**The multiple math**:
- One-time revenue businesses: 1–2x revenue
- Mixed recurring/one-time: 2–3x ARR
- Pure MRR SaaS: 3–6x ARR
- Pure ARR SaaS with >100% NRR: 5–10x ARR

Recurring is not just a business model — it is the exit multiple multiplier.

**Fix now**: If you have any one-time revenue streams: offer an annual subscription option. Even 20% of customers taking annual pre-payment changes your NRR trajectory and signals predictability to buyers.

---

### Dimension 5: Transfer Readiness (0–20 points)

| Factor | Status | Points |
|---|---|---|
| Codebase has comments / documentation | Yes/No | +5 |
| Customer onboarding process documented | Yes/No | +5 |
| Tech stack is modern, maintainable, and handoffable | Yes/No | +5 |
| No single point of failure dependent on you | Yes/No | +5 |

**Score < 10**: "Acquirer due diligence takes 30–90 days. Undocumented businesses fail diligence or get discounted significantly. The question every buyer asks: 'Could a new owner run this in 90 days?' If the answer requires you to train them for 6 months, the answer is no."

**Fix now**: Write the "New Owner Onboarding Guide" — a 2-page document that explains: where everything lives, what runs automatically, what requires attention daily/weekly/monthly, who are the key customers/vendors to know. This document is both transfer readiness AND a forcing function to identify your own dependencies.

## The $5K MRR Exit Prep Weekly Habits

These take <30 minutes/week and compound to maximum exit value:

**Monday** (10 min): Update MRR spreadsheet — new MRR, churned MRR, net, cumulative customers
**Wednesday** (10 min): Review support tickets — categorize by type, identify what's manual vs. could be automated
**Friday** (10 min): Founder dependency audit — what did you do this week that only you could do? Write an SOP for one of them.

Monthly (30 min): Platform concentration review — what % of new customers came from each channel?

That's it. 80 minutes/month. Over 24 months, this is the difference between a 2x and a 4x exit multiple.

## Acquisition Market Context (Early Stage)

**Acquire.com / MicroAcquire tier** ($5K–$20K MRR):
- Multiple range: 2–4x ARR ($120K–$960K range)
- Typical acquirers: indie hackers, small operators, buyers seeking "a job with a product"
- What they care about most: churn rate, founder dependency, clean financials
- Diligence timeline: 14–30 days
- Common deal structure: all cash, sometimes earn-out for first 3 months

**What kills deals at this tier** (in order of frequency):
1. Churn >10% monthly (signals broken retention, not a product)
2. Founder = product (unresolvable buyer risk)
3. Single large customer >40% of revenue (concentration risk)
4. Undocumented codebase (requires founder training)
5. No clear acquisition channel (growth depends on founder hustle)
