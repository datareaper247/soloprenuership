# Decision Log
# Every strategic decision, the rationale, and what actually happened.
# The most underrated founder habit: knowing WHY you made each decision.

---

## Why Log Decisions

1. **Prevents revisiting closed questions** — "Why did we choose X?" has an answer
2. **Builds judgment over time** — You see which of your assumptions were right/wrong
3. **Enables course correction** — Kill signals tell you when to reverse
4. **Makes you a better decision-maker** — Reviewing past decisions calibrates future ones
5. **AI context** — Claude uses this to give advice consistent with past strategic choices

---

## Decision Template

```
DECISION #[N]: [Title]
━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━
Date: [date]
Category: [Pricing / Product / Hiring / Marketing / Strategy / Operations / Tech]
Urgency: [Time-sensitive / Normal / Low urgency]
Reversibility: [🟢 Easy / 🟡 Moderate cost / 🔴 Hard to reverse]

THE DECISION:
[What was decided — be specific enough that future-you knows exactly what happened]

CONTEXT:
[What situation prompted this decision?]
[What were the constraints (time, money, team)?]

OPTIONS CONSIDERED:
Option A: [description] — Pros: [list] — Cons: [list]
Option B: [description] — Pros: [list] — Cons: [list]

CHOSEN: Option [A/B/other] — [one-line rationale]

KEY ASSUMPTIONS (what has to be true for this to be right):
1. [Assumption]
2. [Assumption]

KILL SIGNAL: [What data, within what timeframe, would indicate this was wrong]
Review date: [specific date to check if kill signal triggered]

--- OUTCOME (fill in at review date) ---
Review date: [date]
Result: [What actually happened]
Assumptions correct: [Yes / No / Partial] — [details]
Verdict: ✅ Good decision / ❌ Wrong decision / ⚠️ Too early to tell
What I'd do differently: [honest reflection]
```
